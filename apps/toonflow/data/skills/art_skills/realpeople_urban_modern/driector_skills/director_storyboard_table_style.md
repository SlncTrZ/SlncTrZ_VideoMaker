# Storyboard Table Urban Constraints · Live-Action Urban Photorealism · Technique Reference

---

## I. Storyboard Table Positioning

The storyboard table is the director's core tool for transforming script into camera language. The following only provides constraint specifications for the live-action urban photorealistic style at the storyboard table level.

---

## II. Lighting & Atmosphere

- **Lighting Consistency Within Same Scene** — A scene should not have more than two lighting schemes unless there is aclear narrativeturn/twist (e.g., turning off desk lamp → cold moonlight glow)
- **Emotional Palette Binding** — Each scene must bind at least 1 emotional scenario (such as meeting heart-touching/work argument/date sweetness), and maintain main color + accent color continuity within the shot group
- **Light Source Angle Standards** — Front-facinglens/shotdefault 30° angle (softer), side-facinglens/shotadjust perimage needs. When describing light, must distinguish front/side angle differences
- **Cool-Warm Tone & Narrative Stage Matching** — Work/solitude/conflictsegmentmaintain neutral/cool tone; warm tones (warm orange + cool blue background contrast) used after emotionalrising warm
- **Lighting Transition is an Advanced Technique** — Gradual shift from natural light (A) to indoor warm light (B) = day-to-night time passage. Annotate lighting change points in storyboard table

---

## III. Environmental Dynamics

- **Environmental Dynamics Add Breathing Space to Images** — Car lights flowing outside window, computer screenflickering, coffee cup steam, phone screen lighting up. Every 3-4 shots, arrange at least one with environmental dynamics to avoid the image "dying"
- **Urban Environmental Elements Priority** — Environmental dynamics should select elements within the urban worldview: car light flow, raindrops sliding across windows, coffee steam, phone screen turning on/off, curtains slightly moving, elevator numbers changing. Prohibit elements incompatible with modern urban settings

---

## IV. Urban Action Rhythm

- **Urban Actions Should Be Natural** — All character actions default to natural rhythm. Getting up, turning, raising hand should allannotate "natural" or "casual"
- **Prop Interaction** — In urban scenes, use of props such as phones, coffee cups, documents, car keys are natural dynamic assets. Add prop interaction details in scene descriptions to increase life texture
- **Body Language Modernized** — Urban character movements should conform to modern urban temperament: confident gait, casual leaning,casually small gestures (brushing hair, checking watch, biting lip)

---

## V. Camera Movementtaboo/prohibition

- **Prohibit Fast Camera Movements** — Whip pans, fast pushes, handheld shaking conflict with live-action urban photorealistictemperament (unless action/chase scenes)
- **Prohibit Fancy Transitions** — Swipe, rotate, blinds are incompatible with this style
