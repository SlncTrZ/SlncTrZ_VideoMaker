# Urban Style Constraints · Live-Action Urban Photorealism · Technique Reference

---

## I. Tone System & Image Baseline

- **Tone Base** — The entire film uses Cream White (C1), Warm Skin Tone (C2), Premium Gray (C6) as base colors, overall color temperature slightly warm (5200-5800K), saturation medium-low (40-60%), presenting a warm and bright urban life feel
- **Cool-Warm Narrative Contrast** — Warm colors (Amber Warm C7, Cream Pink C5, Metallic Silver C8) serve as visual signals for emotionalrising warm and relationship confirmation; cool colors are used for pressure, misunderstanding, detachment, and othersegment. Cool-warm switching should synchronize with the emotional arc
- **Cool-Warm Distribution Strategy** — Daily and worksegment primarily neutral/slightly cool; intimacy and reconciliationsegment release warm colors; avoidthroughout high warmth causing emotional layer collapse
- **Palette First Principle** — Insegment planning, first bind the emotional scene (meeting/ambiguity/date/argument/reconciliation, etc.), thendetermine/confirm main color + accent color and lighting scheme, avoiding "storyline correct but emotion wrong color"
- **Prohibited Color Range** — High saturation fluorescent colors, strong neon pollution, excessive cyber colors are incompatible with this style

---

## II. Lighting Scheme System

- **Lighting is Narrative** — 6 lighting schemescorresponding different emotionalsegment. In the director planning phase, determine the lighting baseline direction at thesegment level, rather than specifying shot by shot
- **Light Source Angle** — Front-facinglens/shotdefault 30° angle (softer), side-facinglens/shot adjusted per scene and character relationship

| Lighting Scheme | Name | Tone Direction | Applicable Emotion |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Natural Diffusion | Cream white base + even soft light | Daily commute, life flow narrative |
| B | Warm Side Light | Cream warm +partial warm light | Warm interaction, home relaxation |
| C | Ceiling & Ambient Mix | Neutral gray + soft cool white | Workplace rhythm, rational communication |
| D | Window Light Point Source | Amber warm dominant + cool blue background | Night ambiguity, intimacy progression |
| E | Street Neon Glow | Cool background + warm accents | Urban night scene, romantic wandering |
| F | Overcast Diffused Cool Light | Gray-blue primary + warmpartial | Transitionsegment, low-tidesegment |

- **Cool-Warm Light Distribution** — Warm light can be used throughout but needs layered control; conflict/detachmentsegment reduce warm light proportion to avoid conflict between emotion and light color
- **Atmosphere Direction Mapping** — Each scene's atmosphere direction should map to one of the above lighting schemes (A-F), ensuring visual consistency

## III. Texture Direction

- **Ultra-Clear Documentary Feel** — Core of live-action photographic photography: skin texture visible, each hair strand clearly defined, material detailclear and credible
- **Strong Contrast +extreme/ultimate Detail** — As image texture anchor. Not low-contrast light and fresh, not heavy-filter atmosphere, but real, producible cinematic documentary feel
- **Material Authenticity** — Costumes, props, and sets must have real-world credibility: suit fabric, glass reflections, metal objects, wooden furniture, electronic screen highlights should all conform to urban live-action logic
- **Skin Texture** — Retainreal pores andsubtle imperfections, control smoothing intensity, pursuing "real but beautiful"
- **Realism Does Not Mean Flat** — Amplify emotionalappeal through composition, shot size, movement rhythm, rather than relying on exaggeratedspecial effects and excessive post-production

## IV. Urban Scene Spatial Elements

Scene elements unique to the urban worldview and their visual narrative function:

- **Window Frames/Glass/Doorways** — Natural framing composition props, creating spatial layering and character relationship distance
- **Streets/Subway/Elevators/Office Corridors** — Urban rhythm carriers; switching between commuting and pausing can directly map character psychological state
- **Cafés/Offices/Home Living Rooms** — Emotional containers: caféleaning exploratory, officeleaning toward restraint, homeleaning toward safety and relaxation
- **Weather & Time as Transitions** — Day-to-night, clear-to-rain, dusk-to-night can be used forsegment emotional buffer, avoiding hard cuts
- **Turning Points Use Visuals Rather Than Explanations** —priority use visual means (shot sizesudden change, sight line misalignment, spatial separation) to carrymoodturn/twist, reducing dialogue explanation burden

## V. Urban Instruments & Ambient Sound

Sound element constraints under the urban worldview:

### Instrument Selection

- **Piano** — Core instrument for daily, warm, memorysegment, best reflects urban life texture
- **Guitar** — Effective lead for relaxed, street, slow-paced romantic scenes
- **Strings** — Main foundation for emotional rise and emotionaloutburst segment
- **Lo-fi/Light Electronic** — Style enhancer for youthful, urban nightlife, rhythmswitchingsegment
- **Ambient Sound Priority High** — In urban dramas, real ambient sound is key to immersion; music should not overpower dialogue

### Instrument Combination Strategy

| Emotional Stage | Instrument Combination |
|---|---|
| Calm/Opening/Closing | Piano solo or Guitar solo |
| Daily Progression | Piano + light percussion |
| Ambiguity/Romance | Piano + Strings |
| Emotional Outburst/Conflict | Strings dominant + low-frequency foundation |
| Night Scene/Transition | Lo-fi/light electronic + ambient sound |

### Urban Ambient Sound

- **Typical Ambient Sound Layers** — Traffic sound / subway arrival chime / elevator chime / coffee machine sound / keyboard clicks / rain sound / air conditioning background noise / distant city ambient noise
- **Annotate 1-2 Core Ambient Sounds Per Scene**, to assist subsequent sound design. The more accurate the ambient sound layers, the stronger the urban photorealistic texture
