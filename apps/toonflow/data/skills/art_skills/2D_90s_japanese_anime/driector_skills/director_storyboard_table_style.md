# Storyboard Table Japanese Animation Constraints · 90s Japanese Anime · Technique Reference

---

## I. Storyboard Table Positioning

The storyboard table is the director's core tool for translating scripts into visual language. The following provides only the constraint specifications for the 90s Japanese anime style at the storyboard table level.

---

## II. Lighting and Atmosphere

- **Uniform lighting within the same scene** — A single scene should not feature more than two lighting schemes unless there is a clear narrative shift (e.g., turning off a desk lamp → cool moonlight glow)
- **Emotional color palette binding** — Each scene must bind at least 1 emotional context (e.g., heart-throbbing encounter / everyday warmth / nostalgic farewell), and maintain primary + secondary color continuity within the shot group
- **Light source angle specification** — Default 30-45° side lighting, simulating natural light effects. When describing lighting, distinguish between frontal and side angle differences
- **Warm/cool tones matched to narrative stage** — First half (daily life/work/study) maintains warm tones; cool tones (cool blue + warm color contrast) used after nightfall or emotional shifts
- **Lighting transition as an advanced technique** — Gradual shift from natural light (A) to indoor warm light (B) = passage of time from day to night. Mark lighting change points in the storyboard table

---

## III. Environmental Dynamics

- **Environmental dynamics add breathing room to the frame** — Leaves fluttering, clouds drifting, train passing, raindrops falling, candlelight flickering. Arrange at least one shot with environmental dynamics every 3-4 shots to prevent the frame from feeling "dead"
- **Prioritize Japanese environmental elements** — Environmental dynamics should use elements from the Japanese animation worldview: cherry blossom petals falling, leaves fluttering, clouds drifting, trains passing, raindrops falling. Elements conflicting with 90s Japanese anime style are prohibited

---

## IV. Animation Action Pacing

- **Actions should be natural** — All character actions default to natural pacing. Standing up, turning around, raising a hand should all be marked as "natural" or "gentle"
- **Facial expression details** — Micro-expressions in 90s Japanese anime are an important means of emotional expression. Subtle facial changes (smiling, eyes sparkling, slight head lowering) can be added to scene descriptions to increase the "aliveness" of the frame
- **Subtle and restrained** — The demeanor of Japanese anime characters should be subtle and restrained, with emotional expression relying on details rather than exaggerated actions

---

## V. Camera Movement Taboos

- **Fast camera movement prohibited** — Whip pans, quick zooms, handheld shaking conflict with the aesthetic of 90s Japanese anime (unless used for transitions or emphasis)
- **Fancy transitions prohibited** — Wipe transitions, rotations, blinds, etc., are incompatible with this style
