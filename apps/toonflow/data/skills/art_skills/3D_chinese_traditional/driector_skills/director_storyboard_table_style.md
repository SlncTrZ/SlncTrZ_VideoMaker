# Storyboard Chinese Traditional 3D Constraints · Chinese Traditional 3D · Technique Reference

---

## I. Storyboard Positioning

The storyboard is the director's core tool for translatingscript intolens/shotlanguage. The following provides only the constraint norms for the Chinese Traditional 3D style at the storyboard level.

---

## II. Light and Atmosphere

- **Unified lighting within the same scene** — No more than two lighting schemes should appear within a single scene, unless there is a clear narrativeturn/twist (e.g., day to night, clear to rainy and other natural light changes)
- **Emotion palette binding** — Each scene should bind at least 1 emotional scenario (courtly splendor/landscape artistic conception/boudoir gentleness/wuxia solemnity/festive joy/moonlit serenity), and maintain main color + secondary color continuity within the shot group
- **3D rendering lighting norms** — When describing light, clarify volumetric light direction and intensity, ambient occlusion layering, and depth-of-field blur range. PBR material reflection/refraction effects under different lighting are key to visualtexture
- **Cool/warm tone matching narrative stage** — Solemn/melancholy/suppression segments use cool tones (indigo + ink black),prohibited vermillion/golden yellow and other warm tones; warm tones (vermillion + golden yellow + rouge) used only in splendor, gentleness, festive segments
- **Lighting transitions are advanced technique** — Gradual change from window screen diffusion (E) to moonlit cool glow (F) = day-to-night time passage. Mark lighting change points in the storyboard

---

## III. Environmental Dynamics

- **Environmental dynamics add breathing room to the frame** — Petals falling, mist rising, water rippling, gauze curtains swaying, dust particles in volumetric light. Arrange at least one shot with environmental dynamics every 3-4 shots to avoid the frame feeling "dead"
- **Ancient environment elementspriority** — Environmental dynamics should use elements within the ancient worldview: falling flowers, drifting catkins, light smoke, flowing water, wind through bamboo groves, flickering candlelight. Prohibit modern elements
- **3D particles and volumetric effects** — Chinese Traditional 3D environmental dynamics should fully leverage 3D rendering advantages: volumetric light scattering of mist, PBR reflection on water surfaces, physical fallingtrajectory of petals, volumetric rendering of smoke

---

## IV. Ancient Action Rhythm

- **Ancient actions should be slow** — All character actions default to slow speed. Standing up, turning, raising a hand should all be marked "slow"
- **Costume dynamics** — The flowing of ancient costumes (wide sleeves, skirt hems, draped silk) are natural dynamic assets. The drape and flowing effect of cloth simulation in 3D rendering should be reflected in scene descriptions, adding a sense of "life"
- **Posture constraints** — Ancient character demeanor should match the eratemperament: walking steady and slow, movementsimplicit and restrained, avoiding modern body language (such as shrugging, spreading hands)
- **3D animationtexture** — Action transitions should be natural and smooth, reflecting the refined feel of 3D animation. Avoid stiff pose switches, pay attention to natural joint curves and weight transfer

---

## V. Camera Movement Taboos

- **Fast camera movementprohibited** — Whip pans, quick pushes, handheld shaking conflict with the eleganttemperament of Chinese Traditional 3D
- **Fancy transitionsprohibited** — Wipe, rotate, blinds, etc. are incompatible with this style
- **Use 3D spatial camera movement** — Utilize the spatial depth of 3D scenes for slow push/pull or orbit shots, but maintain a steady and graceful rhythm. Prohibit showythrough/flying camera moves
