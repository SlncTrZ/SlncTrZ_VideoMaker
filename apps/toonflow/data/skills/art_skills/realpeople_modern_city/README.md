# Live-Action Urban Visual Guide · Style Description

This guide is crafted for "Live-Action Urban" visual creation. All art prompts, visual specifications, and generated content are strictly limited to the live-action cinematic urban aesthetic system.

---

## I. Core Style Definition

**Live-Action Urban** is a visual style system based on **live-action cinematic photography aesthetics** with **contemporary Chinese cities** at its core. Its defining characteristics are:

- **Live-action photographic texture**: Anchored in live-action cinematic photography, images present real human skin texture, hair detail, micro-expressions, and body language, eliminating 3D modeling, CG rendering, and hand-drawn feel
- **Contemporary urbanworld view **: All scenes strictly limited to contemporary Chinese urban spaces—office buildings, residential areas, streets, shopping districts, subways, parks, campuses, hospitals, and other real urban living scenes
- **Cinematic photography language**: Based on theatrical live-action film photography, emphasizing organic orchestration of natural and artificial light, professional narrative composition, emotional expression through depth of field and focus, and cinematic color grading
- **Urban humanistic warmth**: While maintaining professional image quality, emphasizes the everyday vitality of city life, genuine emotional connections between people, and aesthetic refinement of ordinary moments

### Creative Philosophy & Core Values

The core creative philosophy of this guide is **"Truth is Power"**:

1. **Aesthetic return to realism**: In an era of increasingly prevalent AI-generated imagery, the irreplaceable power of live-action photographic texture becomes the most emotionally resonant visual language. Skin texture, the warmth in eyes, fabric wrinkles, sunlight filtering through hair strands—these real details create a life-like quality that 3D rendering cannot replicate.

2. **Urban narrative vessel**: The contemporary city is the most familiar narrative space for Chinese audiences. Office cubicles carry career dreams, old neighborhood stairwells echo with everyday life, strangers' glances in subway cars, the light of a late-night convenience store—urban spaces themselves are vast narrative vessels waiting to be explored.

3. **Humanistic visual stance**: This guide centers on "people." No matter how grand the scene or how brilliant the light, it always returns to the emotional state and inner world of the characters. Every frame is a gaze at humanity.

4. **The boundary between cinema and everyday life**: Seeks a delicate balance between "cinematic refinement" and "everyday authenticity"—images are beautiful enough, professional enough, cinematic, but not so polished they lose a sense of life; characters are real enough, vivid enough, but not so casual they lose aesthetic quality.

**Core Values:**

- Provide AI visual creators with a systematic, reusable methodology for live-action urban prompts
- Establish a standardized visual creation pipeline from concept to final output
- Lower the technical threshold and trial-and-error cost of live-action urban AI visual creation
- Fill the gap of "systematic live-action urban methodology" in current AI visual creation

## III. Visual Style Positioning

### 3.1 Overall Style Positioning

**"Cinematic Live-Action Realistic Urban Aesthetics"**

Set in contemporary Chinese cities, anchored in live-action cinematic photography technology, pursuing the image quality and aesthetic height of theatrical urban cinema. The overall style seeks a golden balance between "cinematic feel" and "sense of life"—images are refined but not empty, authentic but not careless, aesthetically on-point but not showing off.

### 3.2 Image Texture Levels

| Texture Dimension | Standard |
|---|---|
| **Skin Texture** | Live-action photography level, visible natural skin texture, pores, fine lines, moles,eliminate excessive smoothing and plastic feel |
| **Hair Texture** | Each strand clearly defined, naturally fluffy or draped, clear layering between lit and shadowed areas,eliminate stiff hair clumps |
| **Fabric Texture** | Materialgrain/texturecan be distinguished (cotton/linen weave, wool fleece, silk sheen, leather grain), wrinkles conform to body dynamics and gravity |
| **Environmental Texture** | Walls show signs of use, floors have natural wear, glass has reflections and refractions, metal has oxidation and scratches,eliminate "brand new prop" feel |
| **Light & Shadow Texture** | Organic orchestration of natural and artificial light,eliminate harsh contrast and unnatural noise, pursuing cinematic color science |

## IV. Key Visual Elements

### 4.1 Character Visual Elements

Live-action urban character visuals center on **"real individuals, urban temperament"**:

- **Facial Standard**: Real skin texture, naturallythree-dimensional features, makeupconform to urban aesthetics without overdoing. Supports diverse face types—cool intellectual, sunny energetic, mature steady, sharp capable, gentle healing
- **Hairstyle Standard**: Contemporary urban trendy hairstyles, natural hair texture,eliminate wig-like feel and stiffstyling. Supports shoulder-length straight hair, collarbone-length hair, neat short hair, lazy curls, commuter low ponytail and other urban hairstyles
- **Attire Standard**: Contemporary urban daily wear system—commuter formal (suits, shirts, trench coats), casual daily (hoodies, knitwear, jeans), urban trendy (designer pieces, layered outfits), home wear (comfortable knit loungewear). Fabricgrain/textureclear, wrinklesnatural
- **Expression & Posture**: Micro-expressionsrealfine/delicate (eyes, mouth corners, brows), body languagenatural not artificial,eliminate stiff posing and overly dramatic expressions
- **Character Temperament Dimensions**: Professional capable / Gentle healing / Youthful energetic / Mature reserved / Sharp independent / Down-to-earth / Artistic melancholy

### 4.2 Scene Visual Elements

- **Office Buildings**: Glass curtain walls, open-plan workstations, private offices, meeting rooms, break rooms—the transparency and opacity of glass partitions, cool diffusion of screen light, afternoon sunlight through blinds creating striped shadows
- **City Streets**: Old alleyways, bustling commercial areas, overpasses and underpasses, bus stops, subway commuting—shadows of street trees, motion blur of traffic, the flow of strangers, urban texture of street signs and shop windows
- **Home Spaces**: Old neighborhood stairwells, high-rise apartment balconies, living rooms, kitchens, bedrooms—the warmth of window light streaming in, traces of daily life in household items, the airy movement of curtains
- **Dining Spaces**: Community noodle shops, trendy cafés, late-night convenience stores, night market stalls, company canteens—the steam and aroma of food, condensation on glass, the enveloping warmth of warm lighting
- **Urban Public Spaces**: Park benches, hospital corridors, campus playgrounds, bookstore corners, rooftops—theecho relationship between space and character emotion

### 4.3 Props & Detail Elements

- **Urban Life Props**: Phone (message notifications, screen reflection), laptop (keyboard light illuminating face), coffee cup (steam rising), headphones (symbol of solitude), umbrella (core prop for rain scenes), work badge, takeout packaging
- **Lighting Props**: Desk lamps, streetlights, car headlights, neon signs, screen light sources, candles—all atmosphere creators in urban narratives
- **Emotional Symbol Props**: Old photos, letters, rings, tickets, lunch boxes, potted plants—small items carrying narrative function
- **Season & Weather Elements**: Spring cherry blossoms/catkins, summer heavy rain/cicadas, autumn falling leaves/trench coats, winter breath/snowflakes—seasonal feel enhances the temporal dimension of urban narratives

### 4.4 Camera Language Elements

| Shot Size | Narrative Function | Live-Action Urban Characteristics |
|---|---|---|
| Extreme Close-Up | Emotional climax, key details | Eyes, fingerslight movement, tears, information screen |
| Close-Up | Character emotion, dialogue reaction | Facial micro-expressions, above shoulders, emphasizing emotional transmission |
| Medium Close-Up | Character interaction, action narrative | Above waist, balancing expression and gesture |
| Medium Shot | Character relationships, spatial context | Full body in frame, relationship between character and environment |
| Full Shot | Environmental atmosphere, character solitude/integration | Character relatively small, spatial narrative primary |
| Empty Shot | Emotional buffer, metaphorical pause | No characters, pure environment shot, cityscape |

## V. Prohibited Content (Highest Priority)

This style strictly excludes the following content types to ensure visual purity and consistency:

1. **Non-Live-Action Texture**: 3D rendering/CG modeling/next-gen game engine/animation style/hand-drawn illustration/watercolor oil painting/anime/voxel/low-poly and all other non-live-action photographic textures
2. **Non-Urbanworld view **: Ancient style/ancient costume/traditional Chinese architecture/fantasy/wuxia/xianxia/Republican era/Western fantasy/medieval/cyberpunk/sci-fi future/interstellar space and other non-contemporary urban settings
3. **Non-Contemporary Chinese Context**: Architecture, streets, signage, character styling clearly not of Chinese urban character (such as kimonos, Korean houses, European-style towns, etc.)
4. **Technical Quality Defects**: Low resolution/blurry/noise/deformed/distorted limbs/twisted features/proportion imbalance/cutout feel/plastic feel/excessive smoothing/mask-like face
5. **Aesthetic Violations**: Excessive saturation/dead fluorescent colors/cheap filter look/excessive HDR/messy dirtyimage/deliberately grim tones
6. **Content Compliance Red Lines**: Exposure/vulgarity/suggestiveness/violence bloodshed/illegal content/discriminatory expression/politically sensitive elements

## VII. Usage Instructions

### Prompt Usage Principles

1. **Prefix First**: All generated content must first load the global style anchoring section from the "prefix prompt" to lock the live-action urban realistic texture baseline
2. **Specialtyoverlay**: On the global prefix foundation, overlay character/scene/prop/storyboard specialty prompts as needed
3. **Negative Required**: Every generation must include "negative prompts" to avoid artifacts and style deviation
4. **Iterative Optimization**: It is recommended to fine-tune specialty prompts based on initial results rather than modifying the global prefix
5. **Platform Adaptation**: These prompts arepriority adapted for Doubao-Seedream (image generation) and Doubao-Seedance (video generation); some prompts may need fine-tuning based on platform characteristics
