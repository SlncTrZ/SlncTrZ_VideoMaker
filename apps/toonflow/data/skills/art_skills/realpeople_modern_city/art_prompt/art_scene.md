# Live-Action Urban Scene Image Generation · Constraint Manual

---

## I. Scene Aesthetic Principles

> Live-action urban scenes are not "modeled spaces" but "real places chosen by the camera." No render engine, no textures, no volumetric light parameters — only actually existing light, real materials, and the daily life of Chinese cities.

1. **Space is Narrative** — An empty office is not "empty," it just got off work. A chair placed askew, a half-cold cup of coffee, a document still open on the screen — the space tells its own story
2. **Real Light, Not Rendered Light** — Scene light has only one source: actual daylight (through windows, doorways), actual artificial light (fluorescent tubes, desk lamps, streetlights, neon signs). The color temperature and direction of light must be physically plausible
3. **Used Space** — Real scenes are not tidy. Clutter on the desk, dust in corners, fingerprints on glass, wear on the floor. A space with no traces of living is a sales showroom, not live-action urban
4. **Real Contemporary Chinese Urban Place** — Scenes must have Chinese signage, Chinese city characteristics (shop signs, road signs, architectural style, greenery species). This is not "generic urban," this is "a specific corner of contemporary urban China"
5. **Single-View Full Scene Panorama** — One photo captures the complete information of the entire venue. Wide-angle or standard focal length, full depth of field, frontally showcasing the full scope and depth of the space — not four-view split, not multi-angle collage

## V. Single-View Panoramic Photography Specifications

> Live-action urban scenes only need a **single** panoramic photo — one photo captures the complete information of the entire space. Not 2×2 four views, not multi-angle stitching.

### Frame Composition

| Item | Photography Requirement |
|---|---|
| Space presentation | Single photo completely presents the full spatial scope of the scene. Use 24-28mm wide angle or 35mm, entire venue's depth, width, and height all visible in one frame |
| Composition | Full depth of field large shot — foreground (door frame/furniture/railing/trees/window frame) forms natural frame → midground is venuesubject → background extends to spatial depth/outside window/skyline |
| Depth of field | Full depth of field f/8-f/11, everything from front to backclear. This is not portrait photography — every corner of the scene should be visible |
| Figures | **Strictly prohibit any person, figure silhouette, or body contour.** The scene is empty — but this "emptiness" has narrative: just got off work / about to begin / people gone / everyday frozen moment |
| Aspect ratio | Recommend 16:9 or 3:2 wide format — matching panoramic spacedisplaying horizontal visual habit |
| Color | Driven by actual light source color temperature, no filters applied. Color science referencing ARRI Alexa/Sony Venice cinematic tones |

### How Empty Scenes Narrate

| Type of "Empty" | Visual Cues | Narrative Hint |
|---|---|---|
| Just got off work | Screen off but power light on, chair not pushed back, half-drunk water on desk | Someone was here, now gone |
| About to begin | Chairs arranged, desk tidy, projector on, curtains drawn, water bottles placed | Someone is coming soon, this is the stillness before start |
| People gone | Empty room after move, hooks and nail marks on wall, floor dust outlines | Someone used to live here, now moved away |
| Everyday frozen | Blanket casually draped on sofa, remote and half-drunk tea on coffee table, light from window on floor | Norm of life, just no one in frame right now |
| Late-night place | Empty corridor with only fluorescent lights, quiet between convenience store shelves, platform after last train | City asleep, but space still awake |

## VII. Constraint Rules

### Mandatory

| # | Rule |
|---|---|
| R1 | Must be **single** panoramic photography — single view, not 2×2 four views, not multi-angle, not turnaround |
| R2 | Must use wide-angle lens (24-28mm) or standard focal length (35mm), full depth of field f/8-f/11, complete spatial depth |
| R3 | Light source must be traceable — natural light direction matches time, artificial light source position and color temperature reasonable |
| R4 | Scene must have foreground/midground/background spatial layers, foreground frame → midground subject → background extension |
| R5 | Must have contemporary Chinese urban recognizability — Chinese signage/Chinese architectural style/Chinese city characteristics |
| R6 | Must have usetraces — wall/floor/furniture natural wear, not showroom not brand new not visualization |
| R7 | **Strictly prohibit any person, figure silhouette, body contour, shadow** |
| R8 | Must declare "live-action real photography + non-3D render non-CG" core anchor |
