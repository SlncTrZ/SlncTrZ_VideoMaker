# Live-Action Urban Character Base Image Generation · Constraint Manual

---

## I. Casting Logic — The Person in Front of the Camera

> The starting point of a 3D character is "build a model"; the starting point of live-action urban is "choose a person to stand in front of the camera." The following principles do not start from modeling, but from casting and shooting.

1. **Face is Narrative** — A face is an unwritten story. Live-action urban does not pursue "perfect features," but "features worth looking at" — recognizable asymmetry, story-telling micro-imperfections, a face that can be gazed at by the camera
2. **Everyday Person Priority Over Celebrity Look** — Urban storytelling needs people who "look like someone you'd meet on the subway every day," not "someone who just stepped off a magazine cover." Real faces in bare-faced states take priority overdelicate makeup styling
3. **Posture Tells the Truth** — Real standing posture is not symmetrical — weight on one leg, shoulders slightly uneven, neck naturally leaning forward or back. Prohibit model-like symmetrical stances and military-style standing at attention
4. **Same Person Recognizable Across Angles** — Front/side/back/close-up four angles must present as photographic records of the same person, not four similar people. Key identification points: jawoutline/silhouette, nose bridgetransition, ear shape, hairline direction

## IV. Skin Texture — Skin Under the Camera

> The skin texture of live-action urban is not a render parameter, but the behavior of light on real skin: reflection, transmission, and scattering all depend on the skin's own oil, moisture, pigment, and age.

### Female

| Dimension | Photographic Requirement | Prompt |
|---|---|---|
| Skin tone base | Real Asian urban female skin tone range: cool fair to warm fair, allow slight unevenness (redder cheekbones, darker under-eye area) — this is evidence of reality | Real Asian female skin tone, natural skin unevenness, natural cheekbone flush, real under-eye texture |
| Surface luster | Not matte not oily — natural T-zone oil reflection, natural cheekbone highlight — signs that skin is "alive" | Natural skin luster, real T-zone oil reflection, living skin not modeled |
| Texture visibility | At 50mm lens, 1m distance: pores above cheekbones faintly visible, pores at nose wings clear, fine lines between brows naturally present | Visible real skin pores, real nose wing texture, natural brow fine lines |

## VII. Base Clothing — The Piece That Has Been Worn

> Live-action urban base clothing is not "base layer" — it's "the piece that's been hung over a chair, worn several times, and has the memory of the body."

### Female Base Clothing

Solid color basic top (crewneck T-shirt/cotton shirt/knit cardigan) + basic bottom (straight-leg jeans/cotton pants/knee-length skirt), colors in black/white/gray/cream/navy/khaki and other urban daily basic colors. Fabric visible with natural texture (cotton's soft folds, denim's twill texture, knit's loop texture). No brand logos, no large-area prints, no decorative design.

### Male Base Clothing

Solid color basic top (crewneck T-shirt/Henley long-sleeve/cotton shirt) + basic bottom (straight-leg jeans/cotton casual pants), colors in black/white/gray/navy/khaki and other urban daily basic colors. Fabric visible with natural texture (post-wash micro-wrinkles in cotton, denim fading and wear, natural curling of shirt collar). No brand logos, no large-area prints, no decorative design.

### Unified Dressing Rules

- Base clothing is not "base layer" — it itself is a complete daily outfit, just stripped of all decorative elements
- Clothing has "worn" traces: hem naturally subtly wrinkled, cuffs have on/offtraces, knees/elbows have slight stretch texture
- Coverage: basically covers except face/neck/hands, daily urban wear coverage, no excessive exposure
- Four angles' clothing style, color, and texture fully consistent, presented as photographic records of the same garment from different angles

## VIII. Character Portrait Series — Four-Angle Photography Specifications

> The following is not a 3D "four-view turnaround" but four photographic angles of the same person in the **same studio shoot**. Lighting, clothing, and subject state must present as a continuous shooting record.

### View Definitions

| Position | Angle | Shot Size | Photography Requirement | Prompt |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Left 1 | Front close-up | Top of head to upper clavicle | Face occupies 60%+ of frame. Focal length 50mm or 85mm, shallow depth of field (f/2.8-f/4), focus on pupil. Soft even lighting, natural catchlight in eyes. Complete hairline to collarbone, no crown cropping | portrait closeup, head to collarbone, shallow depth of field, eyes in sharp focus, natural catchlight, live-action photography |
| Left 2 | Front 0° | Full body | Person facing camera, arms naturally hanging or slightly bent at sides. Focal length 35mm or 50mm. Moderate depth of field (f/5.6-f/8), person fully clear from head to toe | front view, full body, facing camera, head to toe complete, live-action full body portrait |
| Right 2 | Right 90° | Full body | Person side to camera, presenting pure side contour. Nose bridge, lip shape, jaw, shoulder-back, leg sideoutline/silhouetteclear distinguishable. Hair side state natural. Complete head to toe | profile view, full body, side angle 90 degrees, head to toe complete, natural side profile |
| Right 1 | Back 180° | Full body | Person back to camera. Presenting back hairstyle, back posture, heels. Natural nape hairline, ear-back flyaways, back collar visible. Complete head to toe | back view, full body, rear angle 180 degrees, head to toe complete, live-action back portrait |

### Frame Specifications

| Item | Photography Requirement |
|---|---|
| Layout | Same frame left to right side-by-side four angles, even spacing, no overlap. Presented as a "casting photo/character reference board" layout |
| Background | Medium gray seamless backdrop paper, value approx. #B0B0B0, no seams, no gradient, no light spots, no shadows. Backdrop paper's slight texture visible, proving this is a real shot not a render |
| Stance | "Stand straight, natural" stance as instructed by photographer — not at attention, not a model pose, but the moment of being asked to stay still during daily standing |
| Consistency | Same person, same shoot, same clothing, same lighting conditions — four angles present continuous photographic records of the same person. Identification anchors: jawoutline/silhouette, ear shape, hairline direction, standing habits |
| Facial expression | Neutral micro-expression — the natural state of the subject facing the camera, not deliberately smiling, not deliberately serious. Eyes have a faint awareness of "looking at the camera" |
| Lighting | Studio standard soft light setup: large front softbox main light + bilateral fill reflectors. Soft even light, clear direction, no hard shadows, no split lighting. Light ratio approx. 1:2 to 1:3, retaining facial three-dimensionality |
| Aspect ratio | Recommend 4:1 or 16:4 wide format |
