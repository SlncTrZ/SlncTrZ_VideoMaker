# Live-Action Urban Scene Derivative Asset Generation · Constraint Manual

---

## I. Derivative Principles

> Live-action urban scene derivative is not "cutting camera positions for a rendered scene" but "the same real place, photographed again by the camera at different positions, at different times, and in different weather."

1. **Space Anchored** — The core spatial structure of the same scene must be recognizable across all variants. Changed angle, changed light, changed weather — but you know at a glance "it's still that place"
2. **Focal Length Narrates** — The same scene shot with different focal lengths tells different stories. Wide angle says "this person's lonely relationship with the city," medium says "look at this corner," telephoto says "what's over there"
3. **Time Period is Mood** — Morning office and late-night office are the same space, two moods. Time changes, light changes, scene's narrative function changes — not just swapping a sky texture
4. **Weather is Story** — Same street, sunny is daily, rainy is melancholy, snowy is romantic or lonely. Weather is the scene's mood filter, but not a filter — it's real optical and environmental change
5. **Single-View Photography** — Each derivative variant is an independent single panoramic photo. Same space as original scene design, but can be different focal length, different time, different weather

## II. Focal Length and Composition Variants — Different Gazes at the Same Space

### Composition Variant Definitions

| Variant | Focal Length | Range | Narrative Function | Prompt |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Wide panorama | 24-28mm | Full scene+surrounding urban environment | Establish spatial positioning, present relationship between space and city, figure here would appear small | 24mm wide panorama, complete space+environment relationship, full depth of field f/8-f/11 |
| Standard panorama | 35mm | Scene complete presentation | Human eye equivalent perspective, objective record of the place, most "honest" composition | 35mm standard panorama, human eye perspective, complete face of the place |
| Medium focus | 50mm | Scene core functional area/most distinctiveclose-up part | Focus on the "heart" of the space — office workstation area, café counter, street intersection | 50mm medium focus, core narrative area of the space |
| Close-up detail | 85mm | Aclose-up part in the space — a window, a lamp, a table | Direct gaze to a narrative detail of the space — that unfinished coffee, that blanket thrown on sofa | 85mm close-up, narrative detail of the space, shallow depth of fieldemphasize subject |
| Same angle different height | — | Viewpoint height change | High angle — fate's gaze; eye level — human perspective; low angle — oppression or grandeur | Low angle upward/high angle downward, maintaining original space structure |

### Focal Length Derivative Rules

| Deriving from Wide Panorama | Keep Unchanged | Changes |
|---|---|---|
| Wide → Standard panorama | Spatial structure, light source logic, time period weather, usetraces | Focal length narrowed to 35mm, framerange reduced but not changing space, perspective closer to human eye |
| Standard → Medium focus | Material texture, light direction, tone, usetraces | Focal length narrowed to 50mm, cropped focusing core area, depth of field moderately shallower |
| Medium → Close-up detail | Material texture, prop positions, light direction | Focal length 85mm, extremely shallow depth of field, background natural blurred,emphasize narrative detail in space |

## III. Time Period Variants — The Makeup Time Gives to Space

> Same space, light at different times turns it into a completely different place. Below is the behavior of real light sources at key time periods.

| Period | Light Recipe | Space's Mood Change | Prompt |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early morning | Low-angle warm white morning light, thin mist in air diffusing, space subtly cool, not fully awake | Quiet, gathering energy, not yet filled with daily routine — "nothing has started yet" | Early morning scene, low-angle morning light slanting in, space subtly cold not yet warm, quiet before anyone arrives |
| Morning | Daylight rising, cool white clear, shadows sharp, materialsclear | Establishment of order — "default" state of daytime scene | Morning daylight, space bright clear, venue in daily operation |
| Noon | Top light as primary, shadows short and thick, if outdoor then materialsreflective strong | Pause feel — lunch break gap, daily peak | Noon top light, space entering daytime pause, midday quiet or brief relaxation |
| Afternoon | Daylight shifting west, warm tone gradually increasing, slanting light through window/tree forming long shadows and light spots | Lazy, time slowing, everything soft in afternoon — "the longest part of the day" | Afternoon slanting light, long shadows crossing space, light spots scattered, lazy warm afternoon |
| Golden hour | Very warm low-angle gold orange light, long shadows toextreme, everything surfaces gilded | Precious — the most treasured light of the day, fleeting warmth | Golden hour, warm gold slanting light overflowing space, all surfaces taking on gold edge, fleeting warmth |
| Blue hour | Sky deep blue purple, natural light very dark very cold, artificial lights just turned on, warm and cool coexisting | Transition — day ends, night not yet fully taken over, poetic shortest moment | Blue hour, sky deep blue purple through doors and windows, artificial lights just beginning, warm and cool light coexisting |
| Late night | Only artificial light — a desk lamp, streetlight outside window, screen's cool light | Lonely or intimate — world quiet, space belongs to one person (or to no one) | Late night scene, only desk lamp/outside streetlight single warm light source, most areas submerged in dark, extremely quiet |

### Time Period Derivative Rules

| Deriving from Base Period | Keep Unchanged | Changes |
|---|---|---|
| Daytime → Dusk (golden hour) | Spatial structure, furniture/item positions, building exterior | Light source color temperature warming to 2800-3500K, shadows lengthening, bright surfaces gilded with gold tone, artificial lights gradually turning on |
| Daytime → Night | Spatial structure, furniture/item positions, building exterior | Overall exposuredecreased, artificial lights fully on, neon/streetlights/indoor lights become main source, window from day to night scene |
| Indoor daytime → Indoor late night | Spatial structure, furnishing positions | Only minimal light sources remaining (a desk lamp/outside streetlight), large areas submerged in dark, intimate or lonely feel |

## IV. Weather Variants — The Mood Weather Gives to Space

> Same street, same window, different weather turns it into different stories.

| Weather | Space Change | Mood | Prompt |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sunny | Sharp light and shadow, materialsclear, colors saturated | Daily, bright, open | Sunny scene, abundant sunshine, clear light and shadow, material textureclear |
| Cloudy/overcast | Soft light no hard shadows, overall slightly cool gray, even light | Restrained, calm, or prelude to oppression | Overcast soft light, no hard shadows, even diffused light, overall slightly gray cool tone |
| Light mist | Distant view natural blurred, near viewclear, air has visible humidity | Hazy, uncertain, poetic | Misty scene, distant view gradually fading into mist, near viewclear, air with visible humidity |
| Light rain | Window glass with raintraces, wet ground with reflections, outdoor metal/leaves hanging water droplets | Melancholy, contemplative, romantic, or story twist | Light rain scene, window glass raintraces, wet ground natural reflection, air cool and humid |
| Heavy rain | Thick rain curtain, outdoor visibilitydecreased, ground puddles and splash, visualization of rain sound | Isolation — indoors is shelter, outdoors is no escape | Heavy rain scene, thick rain curtain outside window, ground puddles splashing, indoor-outdoor isolation feel |
| Post-rain | Everything wet, ground still has puddles, air crisp clear, reflectionsclear, possible sunlight through clouds | Rebirth, crisp clear, emotional cleansing | Post-rain scene, humid air, ground puddles reflecting sky, everything washed clean crisp clear |
| Light snow | Snowflakes falling, slight white accumulation, warm light through snow curtain, visible snowflake trajectory in air | Quiet, gentle, romantic | Light snow scene, sparse snowflakes falling, thin white on ground, warm light through drifting snow, quiet gentle |
| Heavy snow | Snow accumulation covering surfaces, white dominant, visualization of sound absorbed | Lonely or romantic — world simplified to black and white | Heavy snow scene, snow accumulation covering ground and building edges, world simplified by white, lonely or romantic |

### Weather Derivative Rules

| Deriving from Sunny | Keep Unchanged | Changes |
|---|---|---|
| Sunny → Mist | Spatial structure, building exterior, item positions | Add mist layering, distant view blurred, saturationdecreased, light source producing visible beams (real Tyndall effect, not VFX) |
| Sunny → Light rain | Spatial structure, building exterior, item positions | Outdoor add rain strands, glass add real raintraces, ground wet reflective, tone slightly cool, vegetation hanging water droplets |
| Sunny → Snow | Spatial structure, building exterior, item positions | Add drifting snow/snow accumulation, tone slightly cool white, warm light source contrast elevated, branches/window sills snow accumulation |
| Indoor sunny → Indoor rainy | Spatial structure, furnishing positions | Outside window turning into rain scene, window glass with raintraces, indoor light dimmer slightly cool, mood from bright to melancholy |

## V. Single-View Photography Specifications — Shared Across All Derivative Variants

> Each derivative variant is an independent **single** panoramic photo. Not 2×2 four views, not multi-angle collage.

| Item | Photography Requirement |
|---|---|
| Spatial structure | **Same space** as original scene design. Building structure/furniture arrangement/item positions in principle unchanged — what changes is focal length/time/weather |
| Focal length | Per variant type (wide panorama→close-up detail), use 24mm/35mm/50mm/85mm |
| Depth of field | Wide panorama→full depth of field f/8-f/11; medium→moderate shallow depth of field f/4-f/5.6; close-up detail→shallow depth of field f/2.8 |
| Light source | Originates from real light — time period corresponding sun height and color temperature, weather corresponding atmospheric scattering and occlusion, indoors corresponding window light and artificial lamps |
| Figures | **All variants strictly prohibit any person, figure silhouette, body contour** |
| Aspect ratio | 16:9 or 3:2 wide format, consistent with original scene design |
| Color | Driven by actual light source color temperature, no filters applied |

Maintain spatial structure, item positions completely unchanged,
{new period's light: direction, color temperature, intensity},
{new period's space mood: quiet/warm/lonely/poetic},

### Weather Variant

Live-action urban scene derivative photography, same space as original scene {scene name},
Same camera position, same focal length, same time period — only weather changed to {new weather},
Maintain spatial structure, item positions completely unchanged,
{new weather's visual features: rain strands/snow/fog/wet ground/raintraces},

## VII. Constraint Rules

### Mandatory

| # | Rule |
|---|---|
| R1 | Scene spatial structure must remain recognizable across all derivative variants — same place, cannot become another place |
| R2 | Time period variant's light must conform to real physics — sun angle determines color temperature and shadow length, artificial lights switch on/off logically |
| R3 | Weather variant must synchronously adapt material surface real physical changes — glass raintraces/ground puddle reflection/vegetation water beads/snow accumulation |
| R4 | Must be **single** panoramic photography — single view, not 2×2 four views, not multi-angle, not turnaround |
| R5 | Must declare light source origin — where light comes from, what color temperature, what type — cannot be sourceless global illumination |
| R6 | Must maintain original space's usetraces — time and weather derivatives cannot "wash away" the space's wear and age feel |
| R7 | **All variants strictly prohibit any person, figure silhouette, body contour** |
| R8 | Must declare "live-action real photography + non-3D render non-CG" core anchor |

### Strictly Prohibited

| # | Strictly Prohibited |
|---|---|
| X1 | Strictly prohibit spatial structure becoming unrecognizable — changing time/weather/focal length cannot "change the place" |
| X2 | Strictly prohibit "3D render / 3D modeling / CG / UE engine / Blender / PBR material / volumetric light / AO" and all CG terminology |
| X3 | Strictly prohibit "2D hand-drawn / illustration / animation / anime" and all non-photographic media |
| X4 | Strictly prohibit "four views / 2×2 grid / multi-angle / turnaround / orthographic view / blueprint" — single only |
| X5 | Strictly prohibit "ancient style/period/xianxia/wuxia/Republican era/cyberpunk/sci-fi/Western fantasy/otherworld" and all non-contemporary urban scenes |
| X6 | Strictly prohibit "any person/figure silhouette/body contour/shadow/limb" |
| X7 | Strictly prohibit "sky texture replacement/Photoshop composite feel/filter application" — weather change must be real optical and environmental change |
| X8 | Strictly prohibit "light source unknown/directionless global uniform illumination" |
| X9 | Strictly prohibit "time period variant not adjusting artificial light on/off and brightness" — evening must turn on lights, late night cannot be fully lit |
| X10 | Strictly prohibit "weather variant erasing space's daily usetraces" — wall after rain is still mottled, ground under snow still has wear |
| X11 | Strictly prohibit "overexposed dead white/underexposed dead black/no layers" |
| X12 | Strictly prohibit "watermark / text / LOGO / signature / border / AI generationtraces" |
