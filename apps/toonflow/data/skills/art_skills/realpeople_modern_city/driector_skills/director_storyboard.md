# Storyboard Prompts · Live-Action Urban · Style-Specific Technique

---

## Scope of Application

This Skill is dedicated to **Live-Action Urban** style storyboard prompt generation, adapted for storyboard creation of urban drama, workplace growth, youthful healing, street life, urban suspense, and other live-action film and televisionsubject matter.

---

## I. Emotion Before the Camera — Face, Gaze, Micro-Expression

> Emotion in Live-Action Urban does not come from "cyborg light effects" or "render parameters," but from the micro-expressions of real actors before the camera. The following mappings areoriented for the way a camera captures real people.

| Emotional Input | Facial Description | Eye Description | Micro-Expression Supplement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Capable / Calm | Sharp expression, facial linesnatural not tense | Eyes determined, looking straight ahead without wandering | Lip line straight, chin slightly tucked,calm aura restrained |
| Sad / Downcast | Eyes and brows lowered, facial muscles relaxed | Unfocused gaze, looking out window or at ground | Corners of mouth naturally downturned, expression melancholy, notdeliberate |
| Gentle / Healing | Brows and eyes relaxed, face soft | Eyes warm and focused, as if listening attentively | Corners of mouth naturally slightly raised, not a smile, but warmth |
| Stern / Guarded | Facial lines slightly tightened, not exaggerated | Eyes sharp but not excessive, gaze forceful | Jaw tightened, expression restrained, body language defensive |
| Surprised / Moved | Outer eye corners slightly raised, brows naturally lift | Eyes suddenlyfocus, pupils slightly dilate | Lips slightly parted, expression lively not exaggerated |
| Contemplative / Introspective | Expression calm, muscles around eyes relaxed | Gaze distant and unfocused, as if seeing but not seeing | Expressioncalm, possibly one hand supporting chin or fingers lightly tapping |
| Joyful / Lively | Natural crow's feet at eye corners, eyes slightly curved | Eyes bright with light, eye corners showing smile lines | Corners of mouth raised, cheeks slightly flushed, expressionvivid but not exaggerated |
| Tired / Weary | Eye sockets slightly sunken, face overall relaxed | Gazehazy, eyelids slightly heavy | Eyes and brows lowered, possible dark circles, expression relaxed |
| Expectant / Hopeful | Brows and eyes slightly raised, face has restrained small movements | Eyes shining, pupils have light | Corners of mouth slightly raised, face has suppressed smile |
| Resolute / Determined | Facial lines clear, chin slightly lifted | Gaze straight ahead, not avoiding not dodging | Chin slightly tucked or lifted, expression decisive but not fierce |

## II. Light Before the Camera — Light Source Logic & Atmosphere

### 2.1 Natural Light Moments

| Moment | Light Source Behavior | Color Temperature & Tone | Spatial Atmosphere |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early Morning | Low-angle warm white daylight, entering indoors through windows; outdoor sunlight just clearing buildings, long shadows | 3500-4500K warm white, air slightly cool | Quiet not yet awake, morning mist clear, everythingnot yetstart |
| Morning | Sun rising, cool white clear, sharp shadows; outdoor building surface textureclear; indoor window light areamaximum | 5000-6000K neutral to slightly cool | Order established, daily operations, efficient and clear |
| Noon | Top light or near top light, shadows short and dense; outdoor material reflections strong; indoor window light entering from above | 5500-6500K cool tone | Pause feel, midday gap, the apex of routine |
| Afternoon | Sunleaning west, warm tone gradually strengthening, angled light passing through windows or trees forming long shadows and patches | 4000-5000K gradually warming | Lazy, time slowing, the longest stretch of the day |
| Golden Hour | Extremely low-angle warm gold diffusion, long shadows to the extreme, all surfaces gilded | 2800-3500K very warm | Fleeting preciousness, the only light for warm memorysegment |
| Blue Hour | Sun below horizon, sky deep blue-purple, natural light extremely dark and cold, artificial lights just turning on | 8000-10000K sky + warm dot artificial light | Transition, poetry, day ended but night not yet taken over |
| Late Night | Only artificial light — streetlights, desk lamps, screen light, car lights. Most areas sinking into darkness | Warm 2800-3200K / Cool 4000-6500K | Solitude or intimacy, the world is quiet |

### 2.2 Emotional Lighting

| Emotional Tone | Light Recipe | Photography Constraints |
|---|---|---|
| Workplace Restraint | Window cool white key light (5000-5500K), possibly with screen cool light filling face, shadow detailsretain | Moderate ratio about 1:3, spatial depth preserved, glass partitions creating multi-layer reflections |
| Daily Relaxation | Large-area window light diffusion (4500-5000K), softened by curtains, no hard shadows | Low contrast clear feel, shallow depth of fieldhighlight character |
| Warm Home | Warm desk lamp/floor lamp side light (2800-3200K), window light as supplement (if daytime), shadows soft | Warmenvelop, single warm point source primary, shadows warm with detail |
| Urban Vibrancy | Mixed light sources — streetlight warm (2800K) + store cool white (4000K) + car lights sweeping | Warm dominant, cool lightpartial contrast, handheld camera presence |
| Rainy Night Solitude | Wet ground reflecting streetlights (cool base 6000K ambient + warm 2800K patches), rain streaks on window scattering | Cool-warm coexistence, single warm point on face, rest sinking into cool dark |
| Suspense Tension | Hard light side lighting, deep shadows, light ratiosignificantly increased, cool-warm contrast (e.g., cool blue outside window + warm yellow indoors) | Large ratio 1:8+, hard lighttrace outline, shadows pressed but with detail |
| Youthful Healing | Abundant natural light (5000-5500K), large-area diffusion, sky light + ground reflection fill | Low contrast high-key, clear and fresh, shallow depth of field softening background |
| Late Night Vulnerability | Single warm source (desk lamp 2800-3200K or window streetlight) forming island lighting, one side of face bright, one side dark | Minimal light source, shadows rich with detail, ratio up to 1:8 |

## IV. Camera Grammar — Lens Selection & Movement

### 4.1 Focal Length Selection

| Focal Length | Narrative Character | Applicable Scenarios |
|---|---|---|
| 24-28mm Wide | Environmentenvelop character, spatial alienation, urban grandeur vs. individual insignificance | Urban empty shots, rooftopoverlook, alone in a crowd |
| 35mm Slight Wide | Subjective viewpoint, immersive realism, coexistence of person and environment | Street walking, indoor dialogue, daily following |
| 50mm Standard | Equivalent to human eye, objective neutral gaze, most honest lens | Character portraits, two-person dialogue, emotional gaze |
| 85mm Medium Tele | Compressing space, closing emotional distance, background blurhighlight character | Close-ups, seeing from across the street, voyeuristic perspective |
| 135mm+ Telephoto | Isolating character from environment, extreme gaze or voyeurism | Isolated individuals in the city, distant gaze, narrative distance |

### 4.2 Shot Size Selection

| Shot Size | Framing Range | Narrative Function |
|---|---|---|
| Extreme Close-Up | Eyes/lips/fingers/object details | Emotionaloutburst point, key information reveal, sensory focus |
| Close-Up | Top of head to above chest | Character emotion, dialogue reaction, facial micro-expressions |
| Medium Close-Up | Top of head to waist | Character interaction, hand gestures, body language in dialogue |
| Medium Shot | Top of head to around knees | Primary relationship between character and space, multiple people in frame |
| Full Shot | Full body + partial environment | Character's position in space, actionroute |
| Wide Full/Empty Shot | Full space, character very small or absent | Spatial narrative, emotional buffer, metaphorical pause |

### 4.3 Camera Movement

| Movement | Visual Psychology | Usage Scenario |
|---|---|---|
| Fixed | Calm observation, letting time speak for itself | Daily scenes, dialogue, empty shot pause |
| Handheld Slight Shake | Presence, breathing quality, subjectiveintervention | Emotional fluctuation, street walking, intimate following |
| Gimbal Flow | Elegant urban wandering, poetry | Important scene transitions, character entrance, spatialdisplay |
| Slow Push | Gradual attention focusing, emotional approach | Dialoguerising warm, truth approaching, emotionalpushing forward |
| Slow Pull | Pulling back distance, revealing full picture, emotional detachment | Farewell, chapter closing, spatialdisplay |
| Tracking | Walking alongside character, subjective accompaniment | Urban walking, tracking, daily following |
| Pan/Tilt | Gaze shift, spatial relationship establishment | Character gazeguide, relationship between spaces |

## V. Fixed Style Anchor Words

**Photography Anchor (Mandatory):**

Live-action photography, live-action film stills, contemporary Chinese urban, cinematic photography, natural and artificial light orchestration, real color science

**Character Texture (Mandatory when character shots are included):**

Live-action photographic texture, real skin texture pores visible, no smoothing no silicone face, natural hair texture with flyaway strands, fabric texture visible with wear wrinkles, natural micro-expressions, real body language

**Scene Texture (Mandatory when scene shots are included):**

Live-action urban scene photography, real building materials and signs of age (mottled walls/worn floors/glass fingerprints), light source directionclear traceable, natural atmospheric perspective, not showroom not render

**Reference Image Consistency Anchor (Mandatory in reference image mode):**

Maintain character appearance, face, hairstyle, costume fully consistent with reference image, maintain scene style consistent with reference image, maintain unified lighting and color tone, all shots presented as continuous photographic record of the same character

**Style Closing (Fixed):**

Live-action photography, contemporary Chinese urban cinematic texture, cinematic photography image, not 3D not render not CG

**Quality Lock Words:**

Mode A (Chinese) — Default:
Live-action photography high-definition quality, cinematic image quality, no subtitles, no watermark, no title overlay, no UI elements

Mode A (Chinese) — In-frame text scenes (when props like signs, street signs, screens, books contain text):
Live-action photography high-definition quality, cinematic image quality, no subtitles, no watermark, no title overlay, no UI elements, text onscene props such as signs, street signs, screens, and bookscan be clearly distinguished

Mode B (English) — Default:
live-action cinematic photography, real human skin texture, contemporary urban China, cinematic color science, natural light photography, high detail, no subtitles, no captions, no watermark, no title overlay, no UI elements

Mode B (English) — In-frame text scenes:

## VI. Aesthetic Prohibited Items

The following words/styles must not appear in output prompts:

- ❌ 3D render / 3D modeling / CG / UE engine / Blender / PBR material / next-gen modeling / volumetric light / ambient occlusion / ray tracing
- ❌ 2D hand-drawn / illustration / animation / anime / manga / chibi / low poly
- ❌ photorealistic / realistic photography (Live-Action Urban is "live-action cinematic photography," not "realistic rendering")
- ❌ Excessive smoothing / plastic mask / zero pores / silicone face / wax doll feel / AI smooth
- ❌ Ancient style / ancient costume / hanfu / xianxia / wuxia / Republican era / cyberpunk / sci-fi / Western fantasy / medieval
- ❌ Wrong human anatomy / limb deformity / facialcollapse/ruin / proportion imbalance
- ❌ High saturation fluorescent colors / neoncolor overflow / cheap filter feel
- ❌ Non-Chinese contemporary urban empty empty scenes / generic city without Chinese signage
