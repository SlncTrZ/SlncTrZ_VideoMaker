# Clay Constraints · Stop-Motion Clay · Technique Reference

---

## I. Color System and Visual Tone

- **Color base** — The entire film uses cream yellow (C1), clay red (C4), and sky blue (C6) as base colors, with an overall warm color temperature (4500-5000K) and medium-low saturation (60-75%), presenting a warm nostalgic feel of retro stop-motion animation
- **Warm tonesthroughout throughout** — The entire film maintains a warm base, withpartial cool colors (light purple C7, mint green C8)accents to add layering. Warm/cool switches should synchronize with the story arc, notcasualblend
- **Palette-first principle** —segment planning should first bind emotional scenarios (meeting/daily life/adventure/parting, etc.), thendetermine/confirm primary + secondary colors and lighting scheme, avoiding "correct plot but wrong emotional color"
- **Prohibited color range** — High-saturation fluorescent colors, neon colors, dark color systems are all incompatible with this style

---

## II. Lighting Scheme System

- **Lighting is narrative** — 6 lighting schemescorresponding different emotionalsegment, the director shoulddetermine/confirm the lightingkey tone direction at thesegment level during planning, not specify shot by shot
- **Light source angle** — Default 30-45° side light, simulating small theater lighting effects

| Lighting Scheme | Scheme Name | Tonetendency | Applicable Emotion |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Soft Light Diffusion | Cream yellow base + uniform soft light | Daily warmth |
| B | Fantasy Warm Light | Amber warm + magical light particles | Fantasy appearance |
| C | Warm Side Light | Warm yellow main tone +partial warm light | Indoor daily life |
| D | Daylight Diffusion | Natural warm tone + shadow layering | Outdoor nature |
| E | Moonlight Cool Tone | Light blue main tone + warm coloraccents | Night/dream |
| F | Soft Focus Warm Light | Warm yellow main tone +fog effect effect | Memory/flashback |

- **Warm tones throughout the film** — Flexibly adjust according to scene needs; cool tones (light blue + warm color contrast) are only used in night/dreamsegment
- **Atmosphere direction mapping** — The atmosphere direction of each scene should map to one of the above lighting schemes (A-F) to ensure visual consistency

---

## III. Texture Direction

- **Stop-motion clay texture documentary feel** — The core of stop-motion clay realism: visible finger impressions, clear clay wrinkles, obvious material graininess
- **Clay texture + delicate lighting** — This is the anchor point of visual texture. Not smooth plastic, not modern CG rendering, but the handcrafted texture of stop-motion animation
- **Material authenticity** — All costume and prop materials must be credible: clay graininess, fabric folds, wood texture, metal luster. Prohibit "plastic feel" and "smooth CG feel"
- **Clay does not meancoarse** — This style emphasizes the clay texture expression of stop-motion animation (clear texture, finger impressions, clay wrinkles), amplifying emotional impact through lighting and action design, rather than relying on complex special effects

## IV. Clay-Style Scene Space Elements

Unique scene elements of the clay-style world and their visual narrative functions:

- **Miniature scenes/small props** — Natural framing compositionprops, small-scale scenes creating a unique "cute miniature world" layering and spatial depth
- **Warm indoor/handcrafted props** — Natural carriers for negative space composition, handcrafted texture props adding "real tactile feel": wooden table = warmth of home, flower pot = life atmosphere, book = knowledge/growth
- **Lamps/window light/candlelight** — Light source carriers in the clay-style world, warm yellow light = warmth/safety (scheme A/C), moonlight = fantasy/cool tone (scheme E), window light = daily/natural (scheme D)
- **Use empty scene shots for transition betweensegment** — This style has rich scene assets (variants of different times/weather),paragraphtransition (transition)suggestion use sceneemptyshot asmoodbuffer,do nothard cut
- **Use visuals rather than dialogue for turning points** — Prioritize visualmeans (lighting changes, shot scale cuts, empty shot metaphors) combined with action design, rather than relying on dialogue explanations

## V. Clay-Style Instruments and Environmental Sound

Sound element constraints under the clay-style world:

### Instrument Selection

- **Music box** — Core instrument for daily/warm/memorysegment, best expresses the texture of stop-motion animation
- **Accordion** — Relaxed/romantic/streetsegment, the gentle strumming suits the daily atmosphere
- **Stringfoundation layering** — Emotionalsublimation/partingsegment, thefine/delicate pull of strings suits emotional transitions
- **Xylophone/glockenspiel** — Fantasy/magicsegment, crisp tone suits dreamy atmosphere
- Stringfoundation layering can enhance cinematic feel but should not overwhelm

### Instrument Combination Strategy

| Emotion Stage | Instrument Combination |
|---|---|
| Stable/opening/closing | Xylophone solo or glockenspiel solo |
| Daily warmth | Music box + accordion |
| Emotionalsublimation/romance | Strings + music box |
| Emotional stability | Music box solo |

### Clay-Style Environmental Sounds

- **Typical environmental sound layers** — Footsteps (clay texture) / page-turning sounds / tableware collision / wind / clock ticking / fabric rustling
- **Mark 1-2 core environmental sounds per scene** to assist subsequent sound design. The richer the environmental sound layers, the more immersive the clay-style scene
