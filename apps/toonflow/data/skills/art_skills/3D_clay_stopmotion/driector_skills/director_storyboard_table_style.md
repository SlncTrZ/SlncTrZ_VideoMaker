# Storyboard Clay Constraints · Stop-Motion Clay · Technique Reference

---

## I. Storyboard Positioning

The storyboard is the director's core tool for translating scripts into cinematic language. The following provides only the constraint specifications for the stop-motion clay style at the storyboard level.

---

## II. Lighting and Atmosphere

- **Uniform lighting within the same scene** — No more than two lighting schemes should appear within one scene, unless there is a clear narrativeturn/twist (such as turning off a desk lamp → cold moonlight glow)
- **Emotion color palette binding** — Each scene must bind at least 1 emotional scenario (e.g., first meeting heart-throbbing/daily warmth/parting sorrow), and maintain primary + secondary color continuity within the shot sequence
- **Light source angle specification** — Default 30-45° side light, simulating small theater lighting effects. When describing lighting, distinguish the angle difference between frontal and side lighting
- **Warm/cool tones matching narrative stage** — The first half (daily life/work/study) maintains warm tones; cool tones (cool blue + warm color contrast) are used after nighttime or emotional turns
- **Lighting transitions as advanced techniques** — Gradual change from natural light (A) to indoor warm light (C) = passage of time from day to night. Mark lighting change points in the storyboard

---

## III. Environmental Dynamics

- **Environmental dynamics add breathing space to the frame** — Leaves fluttering, candlelight flickering, curtains gently swaying, raindrops falling, magical light particles. Arrange at least one shot with environmental dynamics every 3-4 shots to prevent the frame from feeling "dead"
- **Clay-style environmental elements prioritized** — Environmental dynamics should use elements within the clay-style world: leaves fluttering, candlelight flickering, curtains gently swaying, small props moving slightly, magical light particles, reflecting handcrafted texture and miniature world dynamics

---

## IV. Clay-Style Action Rhythm

- **Stop-motion actions should be slow** — All character actions default to a natural slow rhythm. Standing up, turning around, raising a hand should all be marked as "natural" or "gentle"
- **Clay texture dynamics** — Clay character actions inherently carry a "clumsy and cute" quality. Add subtle movements like finger touches, slight head tilts in scene descriptions to increase the frame's "lively" feel
- **Sweet romance interactions use suggestion** — Eye contact, slight finger touches, fingertip contact when passing objects. Do not write large-scale two-person interactions; break them into suggestive close-up shots

---

## V. Camera Movement Taboos

- **Fast camera movements prohibited** — Whip pans, rapid zooms, quick switches conflict with the stop-motion clay style's character
- **Fancy transitions prohibited** — Wipes, rotations, blinds, etc., are incompatible with this style
