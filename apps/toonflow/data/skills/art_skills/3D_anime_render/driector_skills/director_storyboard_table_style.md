# Storyboard 3D Anime Render Constraints · 3D Cel Shading · Technique Reference

---

## I. Storyboard Positioning

The storyboard is the director's core tool for translatingscript intolens/shotlanguage. The following provides only the constraint norms for the 3D cel shading style at the storyboard level.

---

## II. Light and Atmosphere

- **Unified lighting within the same scene** — No more than two lighting schemes should appear within a single scene, unless there is a clear narrativeturn/twist (e.g., turning off desk lamp → moonlight cool glow)
- **Emotion palette binding** — Each scene should bind at least 1 emotional scenario (e.g., meeting/heart flutter, daily warmth, parting sadness), and maintain main color + secondary color continuity within the shot group
- **Light source angle norms** — Default 45° side-backlight, simulating dusk glow effect. When describing light, distinguish between camera-facing and side-facing angle differences
- **Cool/warm tone matching narrative stage** — First half (daily/work/study) maintains warm tones; cool tones (cool blue + warm contrast) used after nightfall or emotionalturn/twist
- **Lighting transitions are advanced technique** — Gradual change from natural light (A) to indoor warm light (B) = day-to-night time passage. Mark lighting change points in the storyboard

---

## III. Environmental Dynamics

- **Environmental dynamics add breathing room to the frame** — Leaves swaying, clouds flowing, traffic passing, raindrops falling, lights flickering. Arrange at least one shot with environmental dynamics every 3-4 shots to avoid the frame feeling "dead"
- **Urban environment elementspriority** — Environmental dynamics should use elements within the urban worldview: leaves swaying, clouds flowing, traffic passing, neon flickering, curtains drifting. Prohibit elements incompatible with 3D anime style

---

## IV. 3D Anime Action Rhythm

- **Natural actions** — All character actions default to natural rhythm. Standing up, turning, raising a hand should all be marked "natural" or "gentle"
- **Sweet interaction cues** — Eye contact, slight finger touch, fingertip contact when passing things. Don't write large-scale two-person interactions in the storyboard; break them into suggestivepartial shots
- **3D anime fluid feel** — The unique "between-frame fluidity" style of 3D anime; actions should becoherentnatural, avoiding stiff frame skipping

---

## V. Camera Movement Taboos

- **Fast camera movementprohibited** — Whip pans, quick pushes, rapid switching conflict with the 3D cel shadingtemperament (unless for transitions or emphasis)
- **Fancy transitionsprohibited** — Wipe, rotate, blinds, etc. are incompatible with this style
