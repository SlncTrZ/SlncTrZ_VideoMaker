# 3D Anime Render Constraints · 3D Cel Shading · Technique Reference

---

## I. Color System and Visual Tone

- **Color base** — The entire film uses warm orange (C1), sky blue (C3), cherry pink (C5) as base colors. Overall color temperature leans warm (4800-5200K), saturation mid-high (65-80%), presenting a bright 3D anime warmth and healing feel
- **Cool/warm narrative contrast** — Warm colors (warm orange C1, amber warm, warm pink)throughout the entire film as the base tone; cool colors (light blue C3, light purple C6) serve aspartialaccents to add layering. Cool/warmswitching should synchronize with the story arc, notcasual mixing
- **Cool/warm allocation strategy** — The entire film maintains a warm tone base; cool tones used only at night or in emotionalturn/twist segments, creating cool-warm contrast
- **Palette-first principle** — Paragraph planning must first bind emotional scenes (meeting/daily/adventure/parting, etc.), then determine main color + secondary color and lighting scheme, avoiding "story arc is right but emotion color is wrong"
- **Prohibited color gamut** — Dark gray color systems, excessively saturated dirty colors, color systems incompatible with the bright 3D animetemperament are all incompatible

## II. Lighting Scheme System

- **Lighting is narrative** — 6 lighting schemes correspond to different emotional segments. The director shoulddetermine/confirm lighting tone direction at the paragraph level during planning, rather than specifying per shot
- **Light source angle** — Default 45° side-backlight, simulating dusk glow effect

| Lighting Scheme | Scheme Name | Huetendency | Applicable Emotions |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Soft morning light | Warm yellow base + uniform soft light | Film opening, urban morning |
| B | Warm side light | Warm orangeprimary tone +partial warm light | Daily warmth, cafe/home |
| C | Top light + ambient light | Neutral warm + soft cool white | Work/study segments |
| D | Backlight glow | Amber warmdominant + warm pinkaccents | Dusk romance, sweet segments |
| E | Neon halos | Warm orange + cool blue background | Night street scene, romance segments |
| F | Warm soft-focus | Warm yellowprimary tone +fog effect effect | Memory/emotional segments |

- **Cool/warm light allocation** — Warm tones available throughout the entire film; cool tones (cool blue + warm contrast) used after nightfall or emotionalturn/twist. Director can adjust cool/warm switching points based on narrative needs
- **Atmosphere direction mapping** — The atmosphere direction of each scene should map to one of the above lighting schemes (A-F), ensuring visual consistency

## III. Texture Direction

- **Cel shading documentary feel** — Core of 3D anime render: clear outline lines, bright coloring, soft light-shadow layering
- **High-detail materials + soft light and shadow** — This is the anchor point for visualtexture. Not overly smooth, not modern realistic rendering, but the unique cel shadingtexture of 3D anime
- **Rich material details** — Material details of all costumes and props should be believable: fabric fold textures, cartoon-style metal highlights, warm gloss of food. Prohibit "overly realistic" and "rough low-poly feel"
- **Cel shading is not flat** — 3D anime emphasizes cel shadingtexture expression (clear outline lines, bright coloring, soft light-shadow layering), amplifying emotional impact through light and composition design, rather than relying on overly complex effects

## IV. Urban Scene Space Elements

Unique scene elements of the 3D anime urban worldview and their visual narrative functions:

- **Door/window frames/corridors/stairs** — Natural frame composition props, creating spatial depth and layering
- **Streets/parks/sky** — Natural carriers of negative space composition; scene is emotion: long shadows on dusk street = romance, cafe warm light =sense of security, high-rise window view = urban distance
- **Desk lamps/neon/window light** — Light carriers of the urban world, desk lamp = warmth/privacy (Scheme B), neon = vitality/romance (Scheme E), window light = daily/peaceful (Scheme A)
- **Use empty scene shots between paragraphs for transition** — This style has rich scene assets (different time periods/weather/atmospheric variants). Scene transitions should use empty scene shots as emotional buffers, not hard cuts
- **Use visuals + actions at turning points** — Prioritize visual means (lighting changes, scene transitions) combined with action design, rather than relying on dialogue explanation

## V. Urban Instruments and Ambient Sound

Sound element constraints under the 3D anime urban worldview:

### Instrument Selection

- **Piano** — Core instrument for daily/warmth/memory segments, best expresses thetexture of 3D anime
- **Guitar** — Relaxed/romantic/street segments, the gentle feel of strumming suits urban atmosphere
- **String foundation** — Emotionalsublimation/parting segments, thefine/delicate feel of bowed strings suits emotional transitions
- **Electronic sound effects** — Youthfulness/rhythm segments, suitable for modern urban scenes
- Ambient sounds are important elements but should not overpower

### Instrument Combination Strategy

| Emotional Stage | Instrument Combination |
|---|---|
| Calm/Opening/Closing | Guitar solo or Electronic sound effects |
| Daily warmth | Piano + light guitar |
| Emotionalsublimation/Romance | Strings + Piano |
| Tension/Urgency | Electronic sound effects + strings foundation |

### Urban Ambient Sound

- **Typical ambient sound layers** — City traffic sound / Coffee shop background sound / Subway chime / Wind through leaves / Crowd chatter / Footsteps / Elevator sound
- **Mark 1-2 core ambient sounds per scene** to assist subsequent sound design. The richer the ambient sound layers, the more immersive the urban scene
