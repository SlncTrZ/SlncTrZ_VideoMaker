# Ink and Watercolor Spatter Constraints · Neo-Traditional Ink-Wash · Technique Reference

---

## I. Color System and Visual Tone

- **Ink dominance principle** — Ink Black (C1 #1A1A1A) is the structural anchor of every frame. All compositions begin from ink lines and washes; color blooms emerge from the ink foundation rather than covering it. Ink coverage must occupy at least 40% of each frame's visual weight
- **Emotional color blooms** — Each emotional peak is marked by a single dominant color bloom (Vermillion Red C3 for passion, Indigo Blue C4 for melancholy, Ochre Yellow C5 for nostalgia, Jade Green C6 for renewal). Only one bloom color per segment; mixing multiple blooms creates visual noise
- **Warm/cool wash contrast** — Warm washes (Vermillion C3, Ochre C5, Gold Leaf C9, Blush Pink C10) represent yang emotions — joy, love, warmth, vitality; cool washes (Indigo C4, Jade C6, Plum Purple C7, Pale Ink C8) represent yin emotions — sorrow, solitude, mystery, transcendence. The balance shifts with the narrative arc
- **Palette-first principle** — Segment planning must first determine the emotional tone, then select the bloom color and wash scheme. Each scene binds to one primary wash scheme (A-F) before any composition work begins
- **Rice paper warmth** — Warm White (C2 #F5F0E8) serves as the canvas ground tone across all frames. Pure white (#FFFFFF) is prohibited; all white areas must carry the warm rice paper tint
- **Prohibited color range** — Fluorescent colors, neon hues, digital gradient blends, metallic gradients, and photorealistic color transitions are all incompatible with this style. Hue shift tolerance ±10°, saturation shift ±15%, lightness shift ±15%

## II. Wash Scheme System

- **Wash scheme defines emotional atmosphere** — 6 wash schemes (A-F) correspond to different emotional arcs. At the director's planning stage, the wash scheme direction should be determined at the segment level, not shot by shot
- **Ink wash layering as anchor** — All wash schemes must maintain visible brush stroke texture and water bloom edges. Flat digital fills are prohibited; each color area must show the character of wet media on absorbent paper

| Wash Scheme | Scheme Name | Tone Tendency | Applicable Emotion |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Ink Dominant | C1 Ink Black + C2 Warm White + C8 Pale Ink Wash | Solemn, contemplative, tragic, restrained power |
| B | Vermillion Accent | C2 Warm White base + C3 Vermillion Red blooms | Passion, romance, anger, vital energy |
| C | Indigo Night | C4 Indigo Blue + C1 Ink Black + C2 moonlight white | Melancholy, mystery, solitude, night transcendence |
| D | Ochre Memory | C5 Ochre Yellow + C8 Pale Ink Wash + C2 sepia warmth | Nostalgia, memory, autumn, gentle longing |
| E | Jade Nature | C6 Jade Green + C2 Warm White + C4 Indigo accents | Renewal, serenity, nature, healing tranquility |
| F | Plum Twilight | C7 Plum Purple + C10 Blush Pink + C1 ink traces | Romance, dreaminess, twilight, tender intimacy |

- **Wash distribution strategy** — Each segment selects one primary wash scheme (80%+ frame count) and may shift to a secondary scheme at narrative turning points. Warm-to-cool shifts indicate emotional descent; cool-to-warm shifts indicate emotional ascent
- **Atmosphere direction mapping** — Each scene's atmosphere must map to one wash scheme (A-F) before visual planning begins. The table ensures that color psychology supports rather than contradicts the narrative

## III. Texture Direction

- **Brush stroke variety** — Alternate between saturated wash (wet-on-wet blooms), dry brush (rough paper texture exposed), flying white (rapid brush leaving paper gaps), and calligraphic line (controlled, varied-width strokes). No single brush technique should dominate more than 60% of a segment
- **Ink wash layering** — Build depth through translucent ink layers. Darkest areas use 5-7 layered washes; mid-tones use 2-4 layers; highlights expose raw paper. Layer edges must show soft bleeding, not hard digital cutoffs
- **Watercolor bloom** — Allow pigment to spread naturally along water edges, creating soft, irregular boundaries between color areas. Bloom radius should vary from 2-15px depending on emotional intensity — larger blooms for passion, smaller for restraint
- **Spatter technique** — Scattered ink droplets and paint spatter serve as emotional exclamation marks. Use at narrative climaxes, transition moments, and dream sequences. Spatter density correlates with emotional turbulence
- **Negative space as active composition** — Empty paper areas (C2 Warm White) are not background — they are breathing room for the composition. At least 15-25% of each frame must be negative space where ink and color recede to let paper speak
- **Rice paper grain** — A subtle paper texture layer sits beneath all washes, visible in lighter areas. Texture intensity should be strongest in negative space and dry brush areas, faintest in saturated washes

## IV. Ink-Wash Scene Space Elements

Characteristic elements of the ink-wash visual world and their narrative functions:

- **Ink wash background gradients** — Scenes open on graded ink washes from dilute to dense, establishing emotional temperature before any subject appears. A wash that darkens top-to-bottom suggests descending emotion; lightening suggests ascending hope
- **Brush stroke zone markers** — Key emotional zones within a frame are marked by intentional brush strokes (a slash of ink, a sweep of color), distinguishing them from passively filled areas. These strokes function as visual emphasis, equivalent to underlining a word
- **Spatter texture overlays** — At emotional peaks, ink or color spatter overlays the entire frame, breaking through the fourth wall. Spatter size, density, and color correspond to the intensity and nature of the emotion
- **Calligraphic line structures** — Trees, bamboo stalks, mountain ridges, architectural beams, and figure outlines are rendered with calligraphic brush lines — varying width, natural taper, and ink density fluctuation within a single stroke
- **Bleeding color edges** — Where two wash areas meet, edges bleed into each other softly. This bleeding is a deliberate visual feature, not a flaw. The bleed width indicates relationship intensity between elements
- **Negative space areas** — Mist, water surfaces, sky, and empty ground are rendered as untouched or barely-touched paper, allowing the viewer's imagination to fill detail. These areas ground the composition in the ink-wash tradition

## V. Instruments and Environmental Sounds

Sound element constraints within the ink-wash worldview:

### Instrument Selection

- **Pipa (four-stringed lute)** — Core instrument for ink foundation and structural rhythm. The pipa's percussive attack mirrors brush strokes landing on paper — crisp, deliberate, irreversible
- **Guqin (seven-stringed zither)** — Emotional wash layer, providing sustained, flowing tones that evoke watercolor blooming on damp paper. Used for contemplative, sorrowful, and transcendent segments
- **Bamboo flute (dizi/xiao)** — Spatter accent — sudden, bright melodic motifs that punctuate emotional peaks like ink droplets scattering. Xiao (vertical flute) for melancholy spatter, dizi (horizontal flute) for joyful spatter
- **Sparse piano** — Represents negative space and paper silence. Lone piano notes held in the silence between phrases create breathing room, mirroring the visual negative space of rice paper
- **Atmospheric drones** — Low, evolving textural layers that evoke the paper grain substrate. Subtle and continuous, they ground the soundscape as ink grounds the visual composition

### Instrument Combination Strategy

| Emotional Phase | Instrument Combination |
|---|---|
| Calm / Opening / Closing | Guqin solo or Guqin + sparse piano |
| Contemplative / Sorrowful | Guqin + xiao + atmospheric drone |
| Passionate / Romantic | Pipa + dizi + warm piano chords |
| Climax / Emotional Outburst | Pipa rapid arpeggios + dizi + spatter percussion |
| Mysterious / Night / Transcendence | Guqin + xiao + low drone + sparse bells |
| Tense / Conflict | Pipa tremolo + drum accents + guqin low strings |
| Nostalgia / Memory | Guqin + bamboo flute (soft) + single piano notes |

### Ink-Wash Environmental Sounds

- **Typical environmental sound layers** — Brush on paper (crisp, textured stroke sounds) / water drops into ink stone / ink stone grinding (circular, rhythmic) / wind through bamboo leaves / distant temple bells / rain on lotus leaves / stream over smooth stones / paper rustling / charcoal embers settling / single bird call at dusk
- **Mark 1-2 core environmental sounds per scene** to anchor the scene in the ink-wash sensory world. The brush-on-paper sound layer is unique to this style and should appear in at least 60% of scenes, especially during calm or transition moments
