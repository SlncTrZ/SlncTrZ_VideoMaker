# Storyboard Prompts · Ink and Watercolor Spatter · Style-Specific Techniques

---

## Scope

This Skill is dedicated to generating storyboard prompts for the **Ink and Watercolor Spatter** style, a neo-traditional ink-wash aesthetic built on ink dominance, watercolor blooms, spatter accents, and rice paper texture.

## Ink-Wash Visual Vocabulary

| Technique | Visual Description | Emotional Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Saturated wash | Dense pigment suspended in water, blooming across damp paper with soft irregular edges | Emotional intensity, overwhelming feeling, depth of passion or sorrow |
| Dry brush | Brush with minimal water dragged across paper, leaving textured gaps of exposed grain | Urgency, tension, desperation, raw energy, the friction of emotion |
| Splatter | Ink or watercolor droplets scattered across the composition, varied sizes | Emotional punctuation, climax, chaos, release, breaking of control |
| Bleeding | Wet pigment flowing into adjacent wet areas, creating soft blurred boundaries between colors | Emotional blending, intimacy, loss of boundaries, dreaminess, transition |
| Negative space | Untouched paper areas where no ink or color reaches, showing Warm White ground | Silence, breath, hope, emptiness, spiritual space, what is left unsaid |
| Calligraphic lines | Single-stroke lines with varying width, ink density, and natural brush taper | Authority, tradition, elegance, irreversible action, decisive moment |

## Scene-specific Prompt Adjustment

| Scene Type | Prompt Adjustment Direction |
|---|---|
| Outdoor (nature / city) | Lead with atmospheric wash (sky/background as emotional ground), then layer foreground elements with calligraphic lines. Use Jade Green or Ochre Yellow wash as base. Add spatter for wind/rain emotional overlay |
| Indoor (room / interior) | Lead with ink shadow zones (corners dissolving into darkness) and Warm White light areas (window/candle). Use dry brush for texture on surfaces. Bleeding edges between light and shadow zones |
| Night (dark / moonlight) | Lead with Indigo Blue deep wash covering 60-70% of frame. Light sources are isolated warm blooms (Gold Leaf or candlelight Ochre). Use dry brush for moonlit texture. Negative space = moonlight reflection |
| Emotional climax (intense moment) | Lead with spatter technique — ink and color droplets across the entire frame. Background wash dissolves into abstract color. Calligraphic lines become jagged and broken. Paper grain shows through thinnest areas |
| Transition (time passing / mood shift) | Lead with bleeding technique — one wash scheme bleeding into another across the frame. Use horizontal color gradients (left wash bleeding into right wash). Add a single calligraphic line or spatter cluster at the transition seam |
