# Chinese-style Anime Constraints · Chinese-style Anime Neo-Chic · Technique Reference

---

## I. Color System and Visual Tone

- **Color base** — The entire film uses the core palette as its base: Moon White (C1 #E8EAF5), Turquoise (C2 #4A9B8A), Vermillion (C3 #C93752), Indigo (C4 #2B4C7E), Gold (C5 #D4AF37), Ink Black (C6 #1A1A2E), Rouge (C7 #A94A5F), Ochre (C8 #965E3E), Gamboge (C9 #F5E375), Grey Purple (C10 #7B6C85), with an overall neutral color temperature (5000-5600K), medium-high saturation (55-70%), presenting the Eastern classical imagery of a full neo-chic palette
- **Warm/cool narrative contrast** — Warm colors (Vermillion C3, Gold C5, Rouge C7, Gamboge C9) serve as visual signals for positive emotions / elegance / celebration; cool colors (Indigo C4, Ink Black C6, Turquoise C2) serve as visual signals for conflict / solemnity / solitude. Warm/cool switching should synchronize with the story arc, not be mixed arbitrarily
- **Warm/cool distribution strategy** — Wuxia solemn / sorrowful solitary segments use cool tones (Indigo + Ink Black + Turquoise); vermillion / gold warm light prohibited; ethereal xianxia / girl's everyday life segments use soft warm tones (Moon White + Rouge + Gamboge); courtly elegance / festival celebration segments use Vermillion + Gold highlights
- **Color palette first principle** — Segment planning must first bind the emotional scene (ethereal xianxia / courtly elegance / solemn wuxia / girl's everyday / moonlit poetry / festival celebration, etc.), then determine the primary + secondary colors and lighting scheme, avoiding "right plot but wrong emotional color"
- **Prohibited color range** — High-saturation fluorescent colors, neon colors, and digitally intense colors are all incompatible with this style. Hue shift tolerance ±8°, saturation shift ±10%, lightness shift ±12%

## II. Lighting Scheme System

- **Lighting is narrative** — 7 lighting schemes correspond to different emotional segments. At the director's planning stage, the lighting tone direction should be determined at the segment level, not shot by shot
- **Cel shading lighting as anchor** — All lighting schemes must maintain cel shading flat texture, with shadows using same-hue darkening, black hard shadows prohibited; volumetric light / depth of field blur and other Japanese rendering techniques are permitted
- **Light source angle** — Frontal shots default to 38.5° oblique soft light (cel shading texture); side shots may be adjusted as needed

| Lighting Scheme | Scheme Name | Tone Tendency | Applicable Emotion |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Soft Clear Light | Moon White base + Rouge slight warm skin tone | Girl's everyday, warm sweet, healing fresh |
| B | Ethereal Elegance | Moon White + Turquoise + Gold rim light | Ethereal xianxia, otherworldly mood, stunning entrance |
| C | Candlelight Warm Shadows | Ochre warm dominant + Ink Black shadows | Night intimacy, affectionate escalation, warm lingering |
| D | Cool Moonlight Glow | Indigo + Moon White cool light | Moonlit poetry, longing solitude, tranquil cool |
| E | Window Gauze Light | Moon White base + side light dappled | Boudoir interior, study daily life, daytime tranquility |
| F | Courtly Radiance | Vermillion + Gold highlights + Ink Black shadows | Courtly elegance, grand celebration, formal dignity |
| G | Cool Solemnity | Indigo + Ink Black + 38.5° side cool light, no warm colors | Wuxia duel, sorrowful solitude, tense atmosphere |

- **Warm/cool light distribution** — Warm light (Ochre warm / Vermillion / Gold) is suitable for positive emotion / elegance segments; wuxia solemn / sorrowful segments should use cool light exclusively. The director may adjust warm/cool switching points according to narrative needs
- **Atmosphere direction mapping** — Each scene's atmosphere direction should map to one of the above lighting schemes (A-G), ensuring visual consistency

## III. Texture Direction

- **Cel shading as anchor** — The core of Chinese-style anime: delicate lines, cel shading, Japanese animation rendering, smooth color block gradients, prohibiting color banding / color spill
- **Delicate brushwork + cinematic quality** — This is the anchor point of visual texture. Not photorealistic photography, not 3D CG, but a fusion of anime Japanese animation rendering and Chinese-style aesthetics
- **Material anime-ization** — All costume, makeup, and prop materials must be rendered with cel shading: silk luster with smooth color blocks, metal reflections with cool glossy lines, jade translucency with internal light color blocks. "3D feel" and "photorealistic feel" are prohibited
- **Skin texture** — Pinkish-white base, cel shading, natural gloss, soft texture, internal light feel, translucent glowing skin. Matte / deathly pale / waxy / oily / overexposed are prohibited
- **Anime does not equal flat** — Chinese-style anime neo-chic emphasizes adding spatial depth and cinematic quality on a cel shading foundation through lighting layers, depth of field blur, volumetric light, etc., rather than a flat, depthless cartoon style

## IV. Chinese-style Anime Scene Space Elements

Characteristic scene elements of the Chinese-style worldview and their visual narrative functions:

- **Gauze curtains / folding screens / door frames / carved window lattices** — Natural framing composition tools, creating layered depth and spatial dimension that "cannot be seen through," presenting delicate carved details with cel shading lines
- **Courtyard / flowering trees / rain curtain / turquoise mountains and waters** — Natural carriers of negative space composition; scene equals emotion: garden full of blooming flowers = relief, sitting alone in rain = solitude, falling leaves swirling = parting sorrow, drifting clouds and mist = ethereal otherworldliness
- **Candlelight / moonlight / window light / lanterns** — Light source carriers of the Chinese-style world; candlelight = warm / intimate (Scheme C), moonlight = cool / solitude (Scheme D), window light = everyday / tranquil (Scheme E), lanterns = celebration / street scene (Scheme F)
- **Flying eaves and brackets / vermillion palace walls / stone arch bridges / classical furniture** — Architectural elements presented with neo-chic aesthetics, rich details, clear cel shading lines, materials with natural wear / patina of age; brand-new flawless "plastic feel" is prohibited
- **Use scene-only shots for transitions between segments** — This style has rich scene assets (different times of day / weather / shot size variants). For segment transitions, use scene-only shots as emotional buffers, avoid hard cuts
- **Use visuals rather than dialogue for turning points** — Prioritize visual means (lighting changes, shot size jumps, empty shot metaphors) over relying on dialogue for explanation
- **Foreground / midground / background layering** — All scenes must have foreground / midground / background, no flatness; outdoor scenes must include atmospheric perspective (distant mountains like eyebrows)

## V. Traditional Chinese Instruments and Environmental Sounds

Sound element constraints within the traditional Chinese worldview:

### Instrument Selection

- **Xiao (vertical flute)** — Core instrument for desolate, solitary, sorrowful segments, highly compatible with moonlit poetry / ethereal xianxia atmosphere
- **Erhu (two-stringed fiddle)** — Emotionally intense, sorrowful, longing segments; the weeping quality of the bowed strings suits emotional outbursts
- **Suona (double-reed horn)** — Segments withintense emotional fluctuations (great sorrow and joy, fateful turns, climax); use sparingly, but when used it's a bombshell
- **Guqin (seven-stringed zither)** — Opening tone-setting / calm segments / xianxia mood, used together with xiao
- **Pipa (four-stringed lute)** — Accent for tense, rapid segments; rhythm driver for wuxia duel scenes, not dominant
- String ensemble as foundation can add cinematic quality but should not overpower

### Instrument Combination Strategy

| Emotional Phase | Instrument Combination |
|---|---|
| Calm / Opening / Closing | Guqin solo or Guqin + Xiao |
| Xianxia / Ethereal / Mood | Xiao + Guqin + light bells |
| Growing Sorrow | Xiao + Erhu |
| Emotional Outburst / Fateful Turn | Suona solo or Suona + Erhu |
| Tense / Rapid / Wuxia | Pipa accents + drum beats + string foundation |
| Girl's Everyday / Sweet | Guzheng + bamboo flute |
| Court / Celebration / Grand | Bronze chimes + Guzheng + string foundation |

### Traditional Chinese Environmental Sounds

- **Typical environmental sound layers** — Cicadas buzzing / insects chirping / babbling brook / wind through bamboo / street vendor calls / night rain on eaves / fabric rustling / wind chimes tinkling / falling petals / faint firefly light sounds
- **Mark 1-2 core environmental sounds per scene** to assist subsequent sound design. The richer the environmental sound layers, the more immersive the Chinese-style scene
