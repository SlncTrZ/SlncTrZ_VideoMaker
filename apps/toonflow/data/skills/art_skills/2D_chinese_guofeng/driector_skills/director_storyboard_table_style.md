# Storyboard Table Chinese-style Anime Constraints · Chinese-style Anime Neo-Chic · Technique Reference

---

## I. Storyboard Table Positioning

The storyboard table is the director's core tool for translating scripts into visual language. The following provides only the constraint specifications for the Chinese-style anime neo-chic style at the storyboard table level.

---

## II. Lighting and Atmosphere

- **Cel shading consistency** — Lighting schemes within a single scene must maintain cel shading flatness consistency, with shadows using same-hue darkening (black hard shadows prohibited), unless there is a clear narrative shift (e.g., candlelight extinguished → cool moonlight glow)
- **Emotional color palette binding** — Each scene must bind at least 1 emotional context (e.g., ethereal xianxia / courtly elegance / solemn wuxia / girl's everyday life / moonlit poetry / festival celebration), and maintain primary + secondary color continuity within the shot group, with colors taken from the core palette (Moon White #E8EAF5 / Turquoise #4A9B8A / Vermillion #C93752 / Indigo #2B4C7E / Gold #D4AF37 / Ink Black #1A1A2E / Rouge #A94A5F / Ochre #965E3E / Gamboge #F5E375 / Grey Purple #7B6C85)
- **Light source angle specification** — Frontal shots default to 38.5° oblique soft light (cel shading texture), side shots adjust as needed by the frame. When describing lighting, distinguish between frontal and side angle differences; lighting rendering must always maintain Japanese anime texture
- **Warm/cool tones matched to narrative stage** — Wuxia solemn / sorrowful melancholy segments use cool tones (Indigo + Ink Black + Turquoise), warm orange / vermillion warm light prohibited; ethereal xianxia / girl's everyday life segments use soft warm tones (Moon White + Rouge + Gamboge); courtly elegance segments use Vermillion + Gold highlights
- **Lighting transition as an advanced technique** — Gradual shift from light filtering through window gauze to warm candlelight shadows = passage of time from day to night. Mark lighting change points in the storyboard table; cel shading color blocks must transition smoothly, prohibiting color banding / color spill
- **Japanese rendering light effects** — Reasonable use of volumetric light, depth of field blur, rim light, and other modern rendering techniques is permitted, but the aesthetic foundation of Chinese-style anime cel shading must be maintained; 3D realistic / CG animation light effects are prohibited

---

## III. Environmental Dynamics

- **Environmental dynamics add breathing room to the frame** — Petals falling, clouds and mist swirling, water rippling, gauze curtains swaying, robes fluttering. Arrange at least one shot with environmental dynamics every 3-4 shots to prevent the frame from feeling "dead"
- **Prioritize Chinese-style anime environmental elements** — Environmental dynamics should use elements from the Chinese-style worldview: falling flowers, floating catkins, light mist, flowing water, wind through bamboo, flickering candlelight, clouds drifting, firefly specks, snowflakes falling; modern elements are prohibited
- **Cel shading dynamic texture** — Environmental dynamic elements (petals / mist / water ripples, etc.) must maintain cel shading flat texture and delicate lines; 3D particle effects or realistic fluid effects are prohibited

---

## IV. Chinese-style Anime Action Pacing

- **Elegant movements with anime feel** — Character actions default to slow and elegant, but may use anime-style exaggeration on keyframes (e.g., sharp motion lines in wuxia duels, hair strands flowing during a girl's turn). Standing up, turning around, raising a hand should be marked as "slow / elegant"
- **Costume dynamics are core visual assets** — The flowing of Chinese-style anime costumes (wide sleeves, skirts, draped silk, ribbons) is a natural dynamic asset. Costume dynamics such as robes / skirts / ribbons can be included in scene descriptions. Under cel shading, costume flowing should have clear lines and distinct layering
- **Posture constraints** — The demeanor of Chinese-style characters should match classical temperament: steady slow walking, restrained and subtle gestures, no modern body language (e.g., shrugging, spreading hands). Anime characters may have slight exaggeration in micro-expressions (crescent eyes, flushed cheeks), but body movements must remain classically restrained
- **Consistent head-to-body ratio** — Characters must maintain a 6-7 head anime-classical proportion in action design; breaking the head-to-body ratio due to dynamic poses is prohibited

---

## V. Material and Texture Constraints

- **Cel shading as anchor** — In all shots, character / costume / prop / scene rendering must maintain cel shading flat texture, with delicate clear lines and smooth color blocks; 3D realistic / CG animation / photorealistic rendering is prohibited
- **Costume materials** — Silk + embroidery + pearlescent fabric textures rendered with cel shading; textures must be ultra-clear, clothing texture must be distinct
- **Prop materials** — Cool metallic luster, translucent jade, clear wood grain, warm porcelain texture, all presented with cel shading + delicate brushwork; realistic material rendering is prohibited
- **Scene materials** — Wood surfaces with signs of use, stone surfaces with weathering marks, fabric with natural folds; materials carry a sense of age but presented with Chinese-style anime brushwork; "plastic-like" brand-new flawless materials are prohibited

---

## VI. Color Constraints

- **Core palette locked** — All shot colors in the storyboard table must be taken from the core palette (C1-C10), with hue shift tolerance ±8°, saturation shift ±10%, lightness shift ±12%
- **Fluorescent colors prohibited** — High-saturation fluorescent colors / neon colors / digitally intense hues strictly prohibited
- **Line color** — Ink black or dark brown; pure black thick lines prohibited
- **Shadow color** — Same-hue darkening; black hard shadows prohibited
- **Overall color temperature** — Neutral 5000-5600K natural light base, contrast 45-65%, saturation 55-70% (full neo-chic palette)

---

## VII. Camera Movement Taboos

- **Fast camera movement prohibited** — Whip pans, quick zooms, handheld shaking conflict with the elegant aesthetic of Chinese-style anime neo-chic; wuxia duel scenes may moderately accelerate camera pacing but must still maintain frame clarity
- **Fancy transitions prohibited** — Wipe transitions, rotations, blinds, etc., are incompatible with this style
- **Realistic camera language prohibited** — Handheld shaking / documentary-style tracking / fisheye lens, etc., conflict with anime aesthetics and disrupt the visual purity of cel shading
