# Storyboard Chinese Cyber 3D Constraints · Chinese Cyber 3D · Technique Reference
---

## I. Storyboard Positioning
The storyboard is the director's core tool for translating scripts into cinematic language. The following provides only the constraint specifications for the Chinese cyber 3D style at the storyboard level, **with the core principle of "Chinese aesthetic core first, cyber elements secondary." Whether in classical ancient scenes or modern urban scenes, the elegant Eastern classical atmosphere and narrative logic must be upheld, and cyber elements must not overshadow the main theme.**

---

## II. Dual-Scene Universal Core Principles (Mandatory for Ancient/Urban)
1. **Style Proportion Iron Law**: In all shots, Chinese core elements (traditional colors, Eastern forms, classical atmosphere, Chinese aesthetics) account for ≥70%, cyber tech elements ≤30%,eliminate pure Western cyber or tech stacking without core
2. **Dual-Scene Adaptation Logic**:
 - Classical ancient scenes: Based on traditional Chinese architecture/costume/etiquette, with cyber elements as narrativeaccents, notdestruction the completeness of the ancient world view
 - Modern urban scenes: Based on new Chinese aesthetics and Eastern traditional core, with cyber elements fused with urban scenes. Must implant grounded Chinese symbols (new Chinese form, traditional colors, Eastern etiquette, Chinese patterns), prohibit pure urban cyber without Chinese core
3. **Rhythm Unity Rule**: Regardless of ancient/urban scenes, the entire film defaults to maintaining agentle soothing Eastern elegant rhythm. Only strong narrative conflictsegment may temporarily break this, and mustannotateclearduration of and trigger logic

## III. Lighting and Atmosphere (Fully adapted for Ancient/Urban Dual Scenes)
- **Uniform lighting within the same scene** — No more than two lighting schemes should appear within one scene, unless there is a clear narrativeturn/twist (such as day to night, clear to rainy natural light changes, real to digitaldreamscape switch); cyber-specific lighting (neon, holographic projection, electronic screen self-emission, prosthetic body light components) must be integrated into the same scene's unified lighting scheme. **In ancient scenes**, neon main color and electronic lightcolor temperature must match ancient light sources (lanterns, candle flames, sky light). **In urban scenes**, cyber light sources must match Chinese base light sources (new Chinese chandeliers, Chinese-style street lamps, building Chinese-style rim light) incolor temperature uniformity, prohibitingcolor noise light intrusion without narrative logic
- **Emotion color palette binding** — Each scene must bind at least 1 emotional scenario, full coverage for both scenes:
 - Ancient basic types: Palace magnificence/landscape artistic conception/boudoir gentleness/martial arts killing intent/festive joy/moonlit quietude

## VI. Camera Movement and Transition Taboos (Fully adapted for Ancient/Urban Dual Scenes)
- **Fast camera movements prohibited** — Whip pans, rapid zooms, handheld shaking, high-speedthrough, strobe jump cuts, first-person fast running camera movements all conflict with Chinese 3D eleganttemperament, **entire film prohibited by default in both ancient/urban scenes**; only within strong narrative conflict (explosion, system crash, life-and-death fight) can extremely short-duration fast camera movement be used in a single shot, and duration must not exceed 20% of total shot length,prohibiteddestruction Eastern eleganttemperament for showmanship
- **Fancy transitions prohibited** — Wipes, rotations, blinds, flash white hard cuts, glitch art code transitions without narrative logic, zoom bounce transitions are all incompatible with this style, **entire film prohibited in both ancient/urban scenes**; only transitions matching Chinese cybertemperament are allowed: light gradient transitions, element matching transitions (ancient flying eaves matching cyber flying eaves, jade pendant matching Chinese access card, lantern matching Chinese street lamp), spatial depth push-pull transitions, slow dissolve transitions, transition rhythm must begentle soothing, matching the overall Chinesetemperament
