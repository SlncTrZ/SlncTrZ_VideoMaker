# Storyboard Ancient Chinese Constraints · Real-Person Ancient Chinese Realistic · Technique Reference

---

## I. Storyboard Positioning

The storyboard is the director's core tool for translating scripts into cinematic language. The following provides only the constraint specifications for the real-person ancient Chinese realistic style at the storyboard level.

---

## II. Lighting and Atmosphere

- **Uniform lighting within the same scene** — No more than two lighting schemes should appear within one scene, unless there is a clear narrativeturn/twist (such as candle blown out → cold moonlight glow)
- **Emotion color palette binding** — Each scene must bind at least 1 emotional scenario (e.g., first meeting heart-throbbing/separation misunderstanding/reunionBuddhist embrace ), and maintain primary + secondary color continuity within the shot sequence
- **Light source angle specification** — For frontal shots, default light source angle is 38.5° oblique (not the generic 45°). For side shots, adjust according to frame needs. When describing lighting, distinguish the angle difference between frontal and side shots
- **Warm/cool tones matching narrative stage** — Sad/solitary/abandonedsegment strictly use cool light (dark greenish cold white),prohibited warm orange/amber; warm tones (warm orange + cool blue background contrast) only used after emotional returnwarm
- **Lighting transitions as advanced techniques** — Gradual change from window screentranslucent (E) to candlelight warm shadow (C) = passage of time from day to night. Mark lighting change points in the storyboard

---

## III. Environmental Dynamics

- **Environmental dynamics add breathing space to the frame** — Petals falling, smoke rising, water ripples, gauze curtains swaying. Arrange at least one shot with environmental dynamics every 3-4 shots to prevent the frame from feeling "dead"
- **Ancient Chinese environmental elements prioritized** — Environmental dynamics should use elements within the ancient Chinese world: falling petals, willow catkins, light smoke, flowing water, wind through bamboo, candle flame flickering. Prohibit modern elements

---

## IV. Ancient Chinese Action Rhythm

- **Ancient Chinese actions should be slow** — All character actions default to slow speed. Standing up, turning around, raising a hand should all be marked "slow"
- **Costume dynamics** — The swaying of ancient Chinese costumes (wide sleeves, skirt hem, draped silk) is a natural dynamic asset. Add clothing/skirt hem dynamics in scene descriptions to increase the frame's "lively" feel
- **Posture constraints** — Ancient Chinese character behavior should conform to theeratemperament: walking steady and slow, gesturesimplicit restrained, no modern body language (such as shrugging,shrug)

---

## V. Camera Movement Taboos

- **Fast camera movements prohibited** — Whip pans, rapid zooms, handheld shaking conflict with the real-person ancient Chinese realistic style's character
- **Fancy transitions prohibited** — Wipes, rotations, blinds, etc., are incompatible with this style
