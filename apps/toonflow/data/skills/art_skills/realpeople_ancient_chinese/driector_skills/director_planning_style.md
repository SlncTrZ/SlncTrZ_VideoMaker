## I. Color System and Visual Tone

- **Color base** — The entire film uses moon white (C1), cool white skin (C2), and dark greenish black (C6) as base colors, with an overall cool color temperature (4800-5200K) and medium-low saturation (30-50%), presenting asadnesscool clear coldhigh-end gray tone
- **Warm/cool narrative contrast** — Warm colors (amber warm C7, pearl gold C3, rosy cloud pink C5) serve as visual signals for narrativeturn/twist, used forpartialaccents in emotional returnwarmsegment. Warm/cool switching should synchronize with the story arc, notcasualblend
- **Warm/cool allocation strategy** — Sad/solitary/oppressivesegment strictly maintain cool tone base,prohibited warm orange, amber and other warm tones; warm colors only released after emotional returnwarm, using warm/cool contrast for narrativeturn/twist
- **Palette-first principle** —segment planning should first bind emotional scenarios (first meeting/rising warm/misunderstanding/reunion/wedding, etc.), thendetermine/confirm primary + secondary colors and lighting scheme, avoiding "correct plot but wrong emotional color"
- **Prohibited color range** — High-saturation fluorescent colors, neon colors, modern color systems are all incompatible with this style

## III. Texture Direction

- **Ultra-clear documentary feel** — Core of real-person realistic photography: pores visible, hair strands distinct, texture details ultra-clear
- **Strong contrast +extreme/ultimate details** — This is the anchor point of visual texture. Not film grain, not ink-wash freehand, but cinematic documentary photography
- **Material authenticity** — All costume and prop materials must becan believable: silk luster, cotton and linen texture, metal reflection, jade warmth. Prohibit "plastic feel" and "CG feel"
- **Skin texture** — Pores, minor imperfections, luster of real skin. Neither over-smoothing nor deliberatelyplacing major flaws, pursuing "real but beautiful"
- **Realistic does not meanplain** — Real-person ancient Chinese realism emphasizesrealcanfilmed visual expression (real light positions, real materials, real action rhythm), amplifying emotional impact through lens and emotion design, rather than relying on spectacle special effects

## IV. Ancient Chinese Scene Space Elements

Unique scene elements of the ancient Chinese world-view and their visual narrative functions:

- **Gauze curtains/screens/door frames** — Natural framing compositionprops, creating "unfathomable" layering and spatial depth
- **Courtyard/flowering trees/rain curtain** — Natural carriers for negative space composition: scene is emotion: courtyard full of blooming flowers = relief, sitting alone in the rain = solitude, falling leaves = parting sorrow
- **Candlelight/moonlight/window light** — Light source carriers in the ancient Chinese world, candlelight = warm/intimate (scheme C), moonlight = cool/solitude (scheme D), window light = daily/quiet (scheme E)
- **Use empty scene shots for transition betweensegment** — This style has rich scene assets (variants of different times/weather).paragraphtransition (transition)suggestion use sceneemptyshot asmoodbuffer,do nothard cut
- **Use visuals rather than dialogue for turning points** — Prioritize visualmeans (sudden light changes, shot scale cuts, empty shot metaphors) rather than relying on dialogue explanations

## V. Ancient Chinese Instruments and Environmental Sound

Sound element constraints under the ancient Chinese world-view:

### Instrument Selection

- **Xiao (vertical flute)** — Core instrument for desolate, solitary, sorrowfulsegment, best at expressingdesolatesorrow gentle 
- **Erhu** — Emotionallyagitated, sorrowful, longingsegment, the crying feel of bowing suitable for emotionaloutburst
- **Suona** — Intense emotional fluctuationsegment (great sorrow and joy, fateturn/twist, climax), use sparingly but nuclear when used
- **Guqin** — Opening tone-setting /stablesegment, used with xiao
- **Pipa** — Tense, urgentsegmentaccents, not asdominant
- Stringfoundation layering can enhance cinematic feel but should not overwhelm

### Instrument Combination Strategy

| Emotion Stage | Instrument Combination |
|---|---|
| Stable/opening/closing | Guqin solo or Guqin + Xiao |
| Growing sorrow | Xiao + Erhu |

### Ancient Chinese Environmental Sounds
