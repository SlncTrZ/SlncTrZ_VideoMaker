# Horror & Supernatural · Director's Narrative Technique Skill Pack

## Introduction

This skill pack provides a comprehensive reference of director's narrative techniques for the **Horror & Supernatural** story genre, covering the full workflow from macro narrative planning to micro storyboard execution. Suitable for any visual style.

## Core Concepts

- **The Unknown is Fear** — True horror comes from "what cannot be seen"; the audience's imagination is far more terrifying than any image
- **Cracks in the Everyday** — The most effective horror is not the monster itself, but the "wrongness" that appears in familiar surroundings
- **Slow Erosion** — Fear is not a one-time scare, but a subtle unease that spreads little by little until there is no escape
- **Humans are More Terrifying than Ghosts** — The deepest horror comes from the dark side of human nature; the supernatural is merely a mirror

## Skill File Descriptions

### 1. Narrative Planning Techniques (`director_planning_narrative.md`)

A reference of techniques used by directors during the **narrative planning phase**, defining the overall planning approach for horror & supernatural in thematic focus, emotional rhythm, scene emotion design, and sound direction.

| Section | Content Summary |
|---|---|
| Thematic Focus & Emotional Core | Fear of the unknown, cracks in the everyday, slow erosion, mirror of human nature |
| Narrative Structure & Rhythm Planning | Unease accumulation, tension and release, blurring reality and illusion, climax twist |
| Scene-by-Scene Emotion Design | Six typical horror segment designs, deprivation of safety, spatial alienation |
| Sound & Music Direction | Lethal silence, low-frequency pressure, unidentified sound sources, sudden stillness |
| Composition & Shot Size Narrative | Spatial negative space as threat, restricted perspective, shot size information control |
| Camera Movement & Rhythm | Breathing camera work, loss of control, voyeuristic perspective, still oppression |

### 2. Storyboard Table Narrative Techniques (`director_storyboard_table_narrative.md`)

A reference of techniques used by directors during the **storyboard table production phase**, defining shot size control, fear construction through camera movement, temporal tension, shot merging, and transition logic for horror & supernatural.

| Section | Content Summary |
|---|---|
| Storyboard Positioning | Description of the storyboard as a core tool for controlling fear rhythm |
| Shot Size Selection | Information-obscuring shot sizes, threat suggestion, restricted subjective field of view |
| Camera Movement Rhythm | Slow approach,out-of-control shaking, sudden stillness, voyeuristic tracking |
| Timing Control | Lengthening fear, compressing scares, silence duration control |
| Shot Merging Strategy | Maintaining continuous unease, atmosphere shot integration, narrative density |
| One-Shot Sequences | Long take suffocation, chase tracking, space exploration |
