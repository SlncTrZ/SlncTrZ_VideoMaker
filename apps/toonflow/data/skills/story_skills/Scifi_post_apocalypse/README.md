# Sci-Fi Post-Apocalypse · Director's Narrative Techniques Skill Pack

## Introduction

This skill pack provides a complete reference of director's narrative techniques for the **Sci-Fi Post-Apocalypse** genre, covering full-process guidance from macro narrative planning to micro storyboard execution. Applicable to any visual style.

## Core Principles

- **Ruins Aesthetics** — The remains of civilization are themselves the most powerful narrative; a collapsed building tells "what happened" more than a thousand words
- **Survival as Theme** — The underlying drive of post-apocalyptic narrative is "staying alive." All choices, morals, and humanity are magnified under survival pressure
- **Humanity Laboratory** — The apocalypse is a natural test of humanity: who betrays, who sacrifices, who still holds onto kindness in despair
- **The Weight of Hope** — The darker the world, the more precious a glimmer of light. A single flower among ruins carries more narrative force than a hundred acres in a garden

## Usage

This skill pack serves as a narrative technique reference for the ToonFlow director AI, automatically loaded during the story creation workflow to guide:

1. **Narrative Planning** — Determine post-apocalyptic core, survival rhythm curve, ruin scene atmosphere, and sound direction
2. **Storyboard Generation** — Output specific shot scales, camera movements, durations, and transition plans based on narrative planning

The two files have clear division of labor and progressive layers, forming a complete closed loop from "why shoot this way" to "how to shoot it specifically."
