# Storyboard Table Narrative Technique · Ancient Xianxia · Technique Reference

---

## I. Storyboard Table Positioning

The storyboard table is the director's core tool for converting script into camera language. Table fields are defined by the director according to project needs (shot number, shot scale, camera movement, duration, characters, events, dialogue, lighting, emotion, transitions, etc.). The following provides technique references specific to the ancient Xianxia narrative type.

---

## II. Shot Scale Selection

- **Wide shot is the foundational shot scale of Xianxia** — Wide shots are used far more frequently in Xianxia narrative than in other genres. A tiny human figure amidst vast heaven and earth themselves convey Xianxia philosophy; don't skimp on wide shots
- **Ink-wash medium shot** — Xianxia medium shots do not pursue information density but the artistic conception of "a person in the frame." The character merges with the environment; mountains, rivers, flowers, and trees are all part of the picture
- **Spiritual aura close-up** — Close-ups in Xianxia focus not purely on expressions but on "spiritual aura": stars swirling in the eyes, spiritual energy flickering at the fingertips, light flowing along the sword blade. Close-ups should have an otherworldly texture
- **Extreme close-ups are extremely restrained** — Extreme close-ups in Xianxia: at most 1 per segment, reserved for "seeing the whole from a tiny part" moments: a teardrop, a petal, a strand of snapped hair
- **Establishing shots should have poetry** — Xianxia establishing shots not only convey the location but also the artistic conception: immortal mountains in a sea of clouds, a lone pavilion under the moon, an ancient temple covered in snow. 1-2 wide shots to complete establishing + artistic conception setup

---

## III. Camera Movement Rhythm

- **Stillness as default** — Over 70% of shots static or extremely slow movement, letting the beauty and artistic conception of the frame speak for themselves
- **Wind-riding tracking** — Flying / leaping segments use smooth tracking, maintaining frame stability, creating a sense of calm wind-riding rather than frantic chasing
- **Slow pan = unrolling a scroll** — Horizontal slow pan showing the full view of landscapes, extremely slow speed, like a scroll unfolding. Suitable for establishing and artistic conception segments
- **Slow push = entering the realm** — Slow push from wide shot to character, the audience "walks into" the world of the frame. Suitable for segment openings and enlightenment segments
- **Slow pull = leaving the world** — Slow pull from character to wide shot, the character merges into heaven and earth. Suitable for parting, transcendence, and ascension segments
- **Rise-fall = ascension / descent** — Camera rising = ascension / enlightenment / transcendence; camera descending = entering the world / falling / returning to the mundane. In Xianxia, rise-fall camera movement is a core narrative tool

---

## IV. Duration Control

- **Leisurely pace for artistic shots** — Landscape empty shots, cultivation static shots get 5-6s of sufficient duration for the audience to "savor" the artistic conception, don't rush to cut away
- **Normal pace for character interaction** — Narrative shots like dialogue and interaction maintained at 3-4s normal pace
- **One move, one shot in combat** — Xianxia combat emphasizes the ritual of one move at a time; each technique gets one complete shot (3-5s), no fragmented cuts
- **Enlightenment shots can be extra-long** — Character meditating / realizing shots can break through the 6s ceiling to 8s, because "enlightenment" itself is a process that needs time. Pair with slow push or static camera position
- **Golden 6-second rule applied flexibly** — In Xianxia narrative, pure artistic shots can be relaxed to 6-8s, but only if the frame has natural dynamics (wind, clouds, water, light changes) to maintain visual interest
- **Time manipulation of "in a single thought"** — At critical decision moments, use slow motion to stretch to 4-6s (the actual action may be only 1s),magnify the character's single thought into a thinking process the audience can perceive

---

## V. Immortal Combat Design

- **Ink-wash priority over realism** — Xianxia combat shots do not pursue "seeing every punch clearly" but convey the "meaning" of the technique: a sword drawn, petals fly; a palm pushed out, mountains and rivers shake. Frame descriptions should focus on artistic conception rather than action details
- **Celestial phenomena respond to combat** — As combat escalates, celestial phenomena change synchronously: technique used → wind rises; techniques clash → clouds surge; ultimate strike → heavens change color. In the storyboard description, synchronously mark the celestial phenomena changes
- **One move decides victory** — The ultimate confrontation in Xianxia is best condensed into "one move": both sides gather power → eye contact → strike simultaneously → for an instant the frame freezes → result revealed. Simplicity is power
- **Eye contact between moves** — Between master-level exchanges, insert 1-2 eye contact shots (medium close-up cross-cutting), using the eyes to convey the understanding of "we both know the next move decides life or death"
- **Don't break up what one shot can cover** — A complete spell execution (starting motion → gathering power → release) should be preferentially completed with one moving long shot, not broken into fragments

---

## VI. Character Relationship Narrative

- **Master-disciple legacy** — The master passing down skills / giving a sword / dying instructions is the most classic transmission shot in Xianxia. Use medium close-up cross-cutting for emotional exchange, close-up for the exchange of keepsakes
- **Dao-partner bond** — Emotional expression in Xianxia is extremely restrained: standing shoulder to shoulder watching the moon without speaking, brushing past leaving a lingering fragrance, meeting again after a thousand years with just a single "you've arrived." The storyboard uses distance and blank space to convey deep feeling
- **Fateful rival mirror** — Fateful rivals use symmetrical composition for confrontation, shot scale progressing from full shot to medium close-up cross-cutting, finally close-up eye contact. Symmetrical confrontation is the ritual of the Xianxia duel
- **Lone traveler narrative** — Xianxia protagonists often have many solo travel segments. Solo travel uses wide shots + back-view composition, not showing the face, letting loneliness convey itself through spatial relationships

---

## VII. Dialogue & Silence

- **Concise words with deep meaning** — Xianxia dialogue should be refined like poetry: one sentence equals ten. "From here on, mountains high and rivers far" is more powerful than "I have to go, take care of yourself, remember to eat"
- **Extended silence as cultivation** — Cultivation / enlightenment / solo travel segments can be completely dialogue-free, lasting 3-5 consecutive shots in pure silence. Silence itself is Xianxia's narrative language
- **Poetic dialogue with empty shots** — If inner monologue or narration is poetic language, pair it with wide shots or landscape empty shots rather than character medium close-ups
- **One line, one shot** — Important lines occupy a single shot, not shared with other lines. Give this line enough space and blank space
- **Dying words are extremely short** — The last words of a dying character in Xianxia should be extremely short (3-5 characters), paired with close-up + fade-out. The shorter, the more weight they carry

---

## VIII. Transition Design

- **Cloud mist transition** — The most classic Xianxia transition: the frame is covered by clouds / mist → clears to reveal a new scene. Natural, artistic, zero disorientation
- **Seasonal cycle** — Use rapid seasonal changes (flowers bloom → leaves fall → snow covers → spring returns) to express the passage of time, suitable for large-span transitions between cultivation segments
- **Water surface reflection** — Cut in through a water surface reflection, ripples disperse into a new scene or memory. Suitable for transitioning to past-life / memory segments
- **Hard cuts within the same scene** — Keep hard cuts between shots within the same scene, clean and crisp
- **Empty shots for breathing between segments** — Between large segments, insert 1-2 landscape / celestial phenomenon empty shots (4-6s) as emotional transition
- **Dreams / past lives use dissolves** — Entering dreams or past-life memories uses slow dissolves; the moment of overlapping frames suggesting the interweaving of two time-spaces
