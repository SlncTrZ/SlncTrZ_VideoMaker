# Narrative Technique · Ancient Xianxia · Technique Reference

---

## I. Thematic Conception & Xianxia Core

### Ancient Xianxia Narrative Points

- **The tug-of-war between heavenly way and human heart** — The core contradiction of Xianxia narrative is the confrontation between "the heavenly way is ruthless" and "the human heart has feelings." Cultivation requires cutting off all emotions and desires, but what moves the audience is precisely the emotions the character cannot let go of — this contradiction itself is the best narrative tension
- **Fate is not the ending but the starting point** — "Destined" is not the narrative ending, but the narrative starting point. Whether a character chooses to accept or resist after knowing their fate is the key to the character arc. The more inescapable the mandate of heaven, the more impactful the act of defying it
- **Lonely pursuit of the Dao** — The Xianxia protagonist's foundation is loneliness: ascending Kunlun alone, keeping watch for a thousand years alone, facing the heavenly tribulation alone. Loneliness is not tragedy; it is the cultivation itself. The narrative must make the audience "understand" the weight of this loneliness
- **Suggested audience takeaway directions** — Desolate majesty / transcendent detachment / lingering regret / enlightening release. Avoid directions that mismatch the Xianxia temperament like "sweet," "lighthearted," or "funny"
- **Great love and small affection** — The scale of Xianxia is "great love" (all living beings / heavenly way / justice), but what moves people is "small affection" (one person's vigil / one unspoken sentence / one keepsake left behind). Great love as the concept, small affection as the touchpoint

## II. Narrative Structure & Fate Rhythm

### Ancient Xianxia Narrative Points

- **Fate foreshadowing runs through the entire work** — Xianxia narrative is suited to plant the seeds of fate (prophecies, celestial phenomena, past-life memory fragments) at the very beginning, keeping the audience in the tension of "waiting for fate to be fulfilled" from start to finish
- **Three-life karma structure** — Xianxia is naturally suited for multi-layer narratives of "past life — present life — next life." The regrets of the past life replay in the present; the choices of this life determine the next. The greater the time span, the heavier the emotions
- **Enlightenment progression** — The protagonist's growth is not "leveling up by defeating monsters" but "progressive enlightenment": from attachment to letting go, from having techniques to having none, from seeking immortality to examining the heart. Each realization should be accompanied by a narrative turning point
- **Use celestial phenomena / seasons as breathing between segments** — Xianxia segment transitions use changes in heaven and earth as natural transitions: spring passing to autumn, sunrise and sunset, clouds gathering and dispersing. More artistic than artificial transitions
- **Tribulation arc** — The rhythmic engine of Xianxia is the "tribulation": heart tribulation (emotional test) → physical tribulation (bodily test) → heavenly tribulation (ultimate test). Each tribulation comes at a greater cost and brings deeper realization than the last
- **"Stillness" in climax segments is more advanced than "movement"** — The highest form of Xianxia's ultimate confrontation is not grand-scale spell collisions, but a silent standoff after all techniques return to one — one sword, one glance, one thought — victory and defeat already decided

## III. Scene-by-Scene Artistic Conception Design

### Ancient Xianxia Narrative Points

- **Describe artistic conception with poetic language** — Don't say "beautiful," say "moonlight on a cold pool, pine shadows slanting crosswise." Poetic descriptions of artistic conception better guide scene art and camera framing
- **Typical Xianxia scene types and design** —

| Scene Type | Emotional Direction | Narrative Technique | Music Suggestion |
|---|---|---|---|
| Entering the World / Appearance | Transcendent + Dazzling | Appearing from clouds, sword-flying, wide shot gradual fade-in | Ethereal flute / xiao, generous blank space |
| Cultivation / Enlightenment | Serene + Focused | Sitting alone on a mountain peak, training under a waterfall, meditating under the stars | Guqin solo or pure natural sounds |
| Love Tribulation / Bond | Restrained + Deep Feeling | Almost touching but not, turning away, exchanging keepsakes | Xiao / xun solo, sad but not sorrowful |
| Misunderstanding / Separation | Desolate + Resolute | Back to back, each stepping onto a different path | Low string music or wind solo |
| Tribulation / Duel | Heroic + Compassionate | Celestial phenomena transforming, one person alone facing heaven and earth | From silence to majestic drum music |
| Ascension / Nirvana | Release + Transcendence | Rising as light, all things reviving, smiling as they fade away | Sanskrit / heavenly sounds fading in and out |

- **Heaven-human resonance is the narrative core** — Character emotions resonate with the natural world:
  - **Calm**: Wind and waves still, sunlight just right, flowers blooming and falling naturally
  - **Sorrow**: Fine rain falling from the sky, leaves fluttering down, moon hidden by clouds
  - **Anger**: Wind suddenly rising, thunder and lightning, earth trembling
  - **Enlightenment**: Clouds parting and sun emerging, heavenly light pouring down, all things shining
- **Space as cultivation realm** — The spatial layers of scenes map the character's cultivation stage: mountain foot (beginner) → mountainside (training) → mountain peak (enlightenment) → beyond the heavens (transcendence). The higher and more open the space, the clearer the artistic conception
- **Shot intent should write "realm" not "image"** — "Using a wide shot to make the audience feel the character's smallness and composure before heaven and earth" is better than "using a wide shot to film a person on a mountain peak." When the artistic expression is clear, the storyboard naturally becomes accurate

## IV. Sound & Music Direction

### Ancient Xianxia Narrative Points

- **Heavenly sounds are music** — The best "music" for Xianxia is the sounds of nature: pine waves, waterfalls, wind chimes, birdsong, distant bells. The richer the layers of natural sounds, the stronger the immortal aura
- **Ancient music with blank space** — Guqin / xiao / flute / xun not fully covering; one phrase one pause, letting the blank spaces between notes convey artistic conception. The blank space of music is as important as the blank space of the frame
- **Avoid modern-feeling sound effects** — Electronic synthesizers, heavy bass, and other modern sound effects are incompatible with the Xianxia temperament. Even in battle segments, use drums / gongs / bells and other traditional percussion as the rhythmic framework
- **Ritual sense of Sanskrit / chanting** — Important segments like ascension, tribulation, and great enlightenment can use Sanskrit or wordless chanting, creating a ritual sense of "transcending the mundane."not exceeding 2-3 instances in the entire work
- **Music emotion model** —

| Emotional Stage | Music Strategy | Coverage |
|---|---|---|
| Daily / Cultivation | Guqin or xiao solo, very low volume | Low |
| Love Tribulation / Bond | Xiao / xun sorrowful playing, restrained | Low-Medium |
| Brewing / Gathering Momentum | Drum beats from far to near, crescendo | Medium |
| Tribulation / Duel | Majestic drum music or sudden complete silence | Extreme |
| Ascension / Transcendence | Sanskrit / heavenly sounds fading away | Medium-High → Diminuendo |

- **"One sound determines everything"** — The most powerful sound effect in Xianxia is often a single sound: one sword ring, one bell toll, one distant thunderbolt. At a critical moment, a single clear sound effect is more impactful than full-rich scoring

## V. Composition & Shot Scale Narrative

### Ancient Xianxia Narrative Points

- **Three core compositions and their narrative functions** —
  - **Landscape ink-wash composition** — A tiny human figure embedded in generous heaven-earth blank space, conveying the Xianxia philosophy of "vast heaven and earth, humans as mayflies"
  - **Cloud mist obscuring composition** — Clouds / mist / haze half-concealing the subject, creating the hazy beauty and mystery of the immortal realm
  - **Layered depth composition** — Foreground branches / medium-shot figure / background mountains layer upon layer; the stronger the spatial depth, the deeper the artistic conception
- **Center composition's sacredness** — Center composition in Xianxia suggests "destiny's call / standing alone between heaven and earth," reserved for ceremonial scenes like ascension, tribulation, and ultimate enlightenment
- **Vertical composition's "heaven-reaching" feel** — Vertical scenes like waterfalls, cliffs, and ascension use vertical composition, emphasizing the momentum of connecting heaven and earth
- **Empty shots are not transitions** — Xianxia's landscape empty shots are themselves narrative: a falling petal = passage of time; a bright moon = longing; a heavy snow = a heart as still as water. Give empty shots 4-6s of sufficient duration
- **Shot scale as realm** —
  - **Extreme wide / wide shot** — Heaven-earth scope, lonely cultivation, sense of fate
  - **Full shot** — Full scene view, ceremonial scene, confrontation positioning
  - **Medium / medium close-up** — Character interaction, dialogue, expression changes
  - **Close-up** — Stars in the eyes, spiritual energy at the fingertips, keepsake texture, spiritual aura focus
- **Function of extreme close-ups in Xianxia** — Not to magnify emotions but to "see the whole from a tiny part": a teardrop reflecting moonlight, a petal falling into a palm, an opponent's face reflected on a sword blade. Extreme close-ups: 2-3 times in the entire work

## VI. Camera Movement & Rhythm

### Ancient Xianxia Narrative Points

- **Stillness as the principle** — Over 70% of shots should be static or extremely slow movement; the temperament of Xianxia is "unmovable as a mountain." The stiller the frame, the deeper the artistic conception
- **Wind-riding tracking** — Segments of sword-flying, cloud-riding use smooth tracking, maintaining frame stability at speed, creating a sense of "riding the wind" rather than "being chased by the wind"
- **Slow pan = heaven-earth scroll unrolling** — Slow horizontal pan showing the full view of landscapes, like a scroll slowly unrolling. Speed should be controlled so the audience has time to "savor every inch of the scenery"
- **Slow push = entering the realm / approaching** — Slow push from wide shot to character, simulating the feeling of "walking into this world." Suitable for opening establishing shots and enlightenment segments
- **Quick cuts are rarely used in Xianxia** — Xianxia combat does not rely on quick cuts for rhythm, but on the "meaning" of each move. Even for spell collisions, use grand complete shots rather than fragmented editing
- **Camera movement as the heavenly way** — Static = stillness / enlightenment; slow push = entering the world / approaching; slow pull = leaving the world / transcendence; slow pan = heaven-earth scroll unrolling; rise-fall = ascension / descent
- **Rise-fall camera movement as Xianxia privilege** — Xianxia is one of the few genres that can extensively use rise-fall camera movement: rising from the ground to the clouds (ascension), descending from the sky to the mortal world (entering the world). The direction of rise and fall is itself narrative
